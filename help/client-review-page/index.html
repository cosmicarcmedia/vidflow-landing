Help / Client approvals

What your client sees

Updated July 10, 2026

When your client opens an approval link, they land on a review page branded as yours. This article walks through that page and what happens with everything they do on it.

#How it works

The review page reads directly from the same approval batch and approval status your agency side sees, there's no separate client-facing copy of the data. Every click, an approval, an undo, a comment, writes straight to that record the instant it happens, which is why there's no submit button and no way for a client to lose unsaved work by closing the tab early.

#No login, your brand

The link opens in any browser. Your client does not create an account, set a password, or sign in to anything.

The page header shows your workspace logo, your workspace name, and the link title. If you have not uploaded a logo, a check mark in your brand color stands in. The progress bar picks up your brand color too. Vidflow's branding does not appear; to your client, this is your review portal.

Set your logo and brand color in your workspace's brand settings. See Set up your brand voice.

#What your client can do

Each content item appears as a card with its media (video player or image), its caption, a status badge, and a comments panel.

  • Approve: the green Approve button marks the item approved. The badge updates immediately.
  • Undo an approval: an approved item's button turns into Approved, click to undo. Clicking it returns the item to Needs Approval.
  • Approve All: while items are still open, an "Approve all remaining items" row at the top offers Approve All.
  • Comment: type into the comment box and click Add comment. Comments stay attached to the item, and you see them when you open the link.

There is no reject button on the review page. A client who wants changes leaves a comment on the item and holds off on approving it. Change requests arrive as comments.

Every action saves the instant it happens. There is no submit step, so a client who approves three items and closes the tab has still approved three items.

Note

Note: The page does not ask reviewers for a name or email. All reviewer comments appear under the name "Client", even when several people use the same link.

#Progress

The top of the page shows a running count ("{n}/{total} items approved") with a progress bar. When every item is approved, a green All content approved! banner replaces the "Approve all remaining items" row.

#How you see the results

Vidflow does not send you a notification when a client approves or comments. Check the Approvals page, and click Open on the link to see per-item status and comments exactly as your client sees them.

In your Content Library, the approval filter shows what has cleared: filter by Approved or Needs Approval, or sort by Unapproved First. See Approval status in your library.

#Get better results

  • Batch content before you send one link, not several links in a week. Every fresh link is another notification your client has to act on. Collect a week or a launch's worth of content, then send one link with everything in it, so review becomes a single sitting instead of a drip of interruptions.
  • Give the link a title your client will recognize at a glance, like "Week 12 Content Review" or the campaign name, not a generic label. It's the first thing they read, and it sets expectations for what they're about to look at.
  • Set your logo and brand color before you send your first link. The review page is the client-facing surface of your review process; a workspace with no logo falls back to a plain check mark, which reads as unfinished. See Set up your brand voice.
  • Ask clients to use comments for anything short of a yes. There's no reject button by design, so if a client is unsure, coach them to leave a comment and skip Approve rather than going silent. A silent, un-approved item is easy to miss; a commented one is not.
  • Check the Approvals page on a cadence, don't wait for a ping. Nothing here emails you when a client acts, so build a daily or twice-weekly check into your routine, especially close to a scheduled publish time for content sitting behind a Require Approval schedule.
