Help / Troubleshooting and FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Updated July 9, 2026

Quick answers to common questions, each with a link to the full article if you need more detail.

#How do I get access to Vidflow?

Vidflow is invite-only during the current beta. Join the waitlist from the marketing site, and you will get access in waves. Once you are approved, you receive an email titled "Your Vidflow access is ready" with a link to sign in at app.vidflow.io using that same email address. See Getting access to Vidflow.

#What counts as an AI generation?

AI generations are a shared monthly pool that includes transcribing an uploaded video, writing clip titles and captions, generating text posts, ideas, scripts, and images, and any other AI-written content. Transcribing a YouTube import does not draw from this pool the same way an upload does, since import quota is tracked separately from the AI pool. Check your plan's allowance and what happens at the cap in Usage limits and monthly allowances.

#Why did my post fail?

A post can fail for a few reasons: the connected account's authorization expired, the platform's requirements were not met (for example, no video on a platform that requires one, or a caption over the limit), or TikTok rejected the compose settings. The post's failure text tells you which platform failed and why. Work through Fix publishing problems in order, it is built as a checklist from most to least likely cause.

#Why are my TikTok posts showing as private?

Beta note

Beta note: While TikTok's app review is pending, every post from Vidflow publishes privately (Only you) on TikTok regardless of the audience you selected. Vidflow shows this on the post and emails you when it happens. This clears up once TikTok approves the app; no action is needed on your end.

#Why are my clips taking a long time?

Most clip runs finish within a few minutes of clicking Generate Clips. If nothing has appeared in your Content Library after about 30 minutes, re-run the generation rather than waiting longer, since a stuck run does not always surface an error on screen. See Clips troubleshooting.

#Do my clients need a Vidflow login to approve content?

No. Approval links are public and tokenized: your client opens the URL and reviews content with no account and no sign-in. The page shows your logo and brand color, not Vidflow's. See What your client sees.

#Do captions work differently on each platform?

Yes. Each platform enforces its own character limit (for example, X text caps at 280 characters and Instagram captions cap at 2200), and some platforms need extra settings, like TikTok's audience and disclosure controls. Turning on per-platform captions, included on the Pro and Agency plans, lets you write a different caption for each platform instead of reusing one caption everywhere. See Post now and Magic Text Posts.

#Can I run multiple brands from one account?

Yes. Each brand lives in its own workspace, with its own content, connected accounts, brand voice, and approvals, fully isolated from your other brands. Your first workspace is free on every plan; running more than one at a time requires the Agency plan. See Running multiple brands.

#What happens when I hit my monthly limit?

Vidflow blocks further AI generations, clip generations, or episode uploads for that metric until the next calendar month resets it, or until you upgrade your plan. You will see a message naming which limit you hit and pointing you to the Billing page. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.

#Can I cancel and get a refund?

You can cancel anytime from the billing portal, and you keep paid access through the end of your current billing period. If you want a refund of your very first charge, email support@vidflow.io within 14 days of that charge. After 14 days, charges are non-refundable except where the law requires otherwise. See Manage your subscription.

#How do I delete my data?

Disconnecting a platform revokes Vidflow's access to it immediately, and deleting a workspace removes all its content. For full account deletion, or a formal data deletion request, email support@vidflow.io or privacy@vidflow.io. See Your data and privacy.

#Which platforms publish automatically on a schedule?

Content on a schedule or queue auto-publishes to X, LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube. Older queue posts created before this dispatcher only auto-publish to X and LinkedIn; any other platform on one of those older posts needs a manual publish from the Library. See Post statuses and failed posts.

#Why can't I invite my team?

Team access is seat-based: each plan includes a set number of working seats and the owner uses one, so adding Admin or Brand Manager teammates starts on the Pro plan. Client reviewers who only need to approve content are always free and unlimited, and never need an invite at all, since they use the approval link instead. See Invite team members.

#Why is Flows greyed out or unavailable?

Flows, the automation builder, requires a Pro or Agency plan. On plans below that, the feature is locked behind an upgrade prompt. See Flows overview.

#Why does OpusClip say it isn't connected?

The OpusClip clip engine needs its own API key connected per workspace, from your own OpusClip account. Vidflow does not provide a shared key. Connect yours in Workspace Settings > Connections, or switch to the Native engine, which needs no key. See Using OpusClip with Vidflow.
