What is Vidflow?

Updated July 9, 2026

Vidflow turns one long-form video into clips, captions, and text posts in your brand voice, then schedules everything across your channels, with client approval from one shareable link.

#The core idea

You upload or import a single long-form video or audio file. Vidflow calls that source an episode. From that one episode, Vidflow can generate short clips, quote and insight posts, blog articles, and images, all written to sound like your brand rather than a generic AI voice. From there you schedule the pieces across your connected accounts or send them to a client for approval first.

The goal is one upload in, a full week (or month) of content out.

#Who it is for

Vidflow is built for video-first creators, podcasters, coaches, and personal brands who record long-form content and need it turned into a steady stream of shorter posts. It also works for agencies running content for multiple client brands at once, since each brand gets its own isolated workspace for content, accounts, voice, and approvals.

#Core concepts

A few terms come up everywhere in Vidflow:

  • Workspace: one workspace holds one brand's episodes, clips, posts, connected social accounts, brand voice, and approval links. Agencies running several brands create one workspace per brand. See Workspaces and brand spaces.
  • Episode: the long-form source video or audio file you upload or import. Episodes get transcribed automatically, and that transcript powers everything generated from them.
  • Content Library: where every piece of content lives, both episodes and the clips, posts, images, and articles generated from them.
  • Posts and clips: the short-form pieces Vidflow generates from an episode, ready to edit, schedule, or send for approval.

#What Vidflow is not

Vidflow does not replace your camera or microphone. You still record the source video. Vidflow picks up from there: transcribing it, cutting it into clips, writing the text posts, and getting everything published on a schedule.

#Getting started

Access to Vidflow is invite-only during the current beta. If you already have access, the fastest way to see the whole workflow is the quickstart guide.
