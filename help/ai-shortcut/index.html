Get the most out of Vidflow by looping in your AI

Updated July 10, 2026

Setting up a brand voice from scratch means typing out how you sound, who you talk to, and what your content looks like. Most people already have an AI assistant that knows a lot of that about them already. The AI Shortcut lets that assistant do the typing.

#Why loop in your AI

If you already talk to ChatGPT, Claude, or another assistant about your business, it likely has a running picture of your brand voice, your audience, and what you post about. Instead of starting from a blank field, you hand it a prompt built for Vidflow, let it ask you a couple of quick questions to fill in any gaps, and bring the answer back.

#The one-paste shortcut

  1. On the setup wizard, choose Use my AI to fill this out.
  2. Click Copy prompt. This copies a ready-made prompt written specifically for Vidflow's brand profile format.
  3. Paste the prompt into ChatGPT, Claude, or whichever AI assistant you use.
  4. Answer the handful of short questions it asks. If it already knows your business, it may only ask one or two, or skip straight to the answer.
  5. Copy the response your AI gives back, in full, including the code block.
  6. Paste it into the box on the Vidflow panel.

As soon as you paste something, Vidflow reads it and shows a live preview: a checklist of every field it found, with the actual value underneath so you can see exactly what is about to be filled in. Click Fill my profile to apply it.

#What each field means and how Vidflow uses it

  • Brand name, industry, and target audience set the basic context every generator reads before writing anything.
  • Voice (tone, vocabulary, sentence structure, content style, common phrases, and topics) is the same shape Vidflow already builds when it analyzes your YouTube channel. It flows straight into captions, posts, and scripts so they sound like you instead of a generic assistant.
  • Brand voice guidelines is free-text direction, phrases to lean into, things to avoid, anything a style guide would normally cover.
  • Image style tells the image generator what visual look to aim for on quote cards and generated graphics.
  • Words and phrases to avoid get folded into your brand voice guidelines so the AI knows what to steer clear of.

Nothing is applied until you click Fill my profile, and every field stays editable afterward. Nothing here is final until you say so.

#If the paste does not parse

Sometimes an AI assistant adds extra commentary around the code block, or the formatting comes through a little off. Vidflow tries hard to read through that, stripping stray text and fixing common formatting slips before giving up. If it still cannot make sense of what was pasted, you will see a plain-language note explaining that, with two options:

  • Try the prompt again. Re-copy the prompt and paste a fresh response.
  • I'll fill it in myself. Drop straight into the manual wizard fields. Nothing is lost, and you can still use the AI Shortcut again later from the same screen.

#Refining later

Everything the AI Shortcut fills in lives in the same place the manual wizard writes to: your workspace's Brand Settings. Open Workspace Settings, go to the Brand and AI & Voice tabs, and edit any field directly. You can also rebuild your voice profile from a YouTube channel or fresh sample text at any time, the AI Shortcut is a fast starting point, not a one-time setting.
