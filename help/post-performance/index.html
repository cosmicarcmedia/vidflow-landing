Help / Analytics

Post performance

Updated July 10, 2026

The Post Performance table on the Analytics page shows how your individual posts are doing on the platforms where Vidflow can read that data back, not just how much you published.

#How it works

After a post publishes through Vidflow, a background process checks in with each supported platform roughly every 6 hours and pulls its current view, like, comment, and share counts. Each pull overwrites that day's numbers rather than piling up duplicate rows, so what you see is always each post's latest count for that day, not a raw event log. Metrics are collected only for posts Vidflow actually published; anything you posted directly on a platform, outside Vidflow, has no way to be matched back to a library item.

#What's collected

  • Views: total views or impressions, where the platform exposes that number.
  • Likes: likes or reactions.
  • Comments: comment count.
  • Shares: shares, retweets and quotes on X, or reposts, where the platform exposes that number.

Not every platform reports every metric. For example, YouTube's public API does not expose a share count, and Facebook post-level view counts require a separate permission Vidflow does not request yet, so those individual numbers read as 0 rather than being estimated.

#Which platforms are supported

  • Supported today: X, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook.
  • Not yet supported: TikTok and LinkedIn. Vidflow can publish to both, but neither platform's API currently gives this app a reliable way to read metrics back for a specific post, so their posts are excluded from this table rather than shown with fake or guessed numbers.

#Reading the table

  1. Open Analytics from the app rail and scroll to Post Performance.
  2. Four totals across the top (Views, Likes, Comments, Shares) sum every tracked post, regardless of the Analytics period selector above.
  3. The table lists one row per published post per platform, with its title and platform.
  4. Click a column header (Views, Likes, Comments, Shares) to sort the table by that metric, highest first.

#When metrics appear

A freshly published post does not have metrics right away. Expect numbers to show up within about 6 hours of publishing, once the next polling pass runs. Until then, or until you've published anything trackable, the section reads "No post metrics yet."

#Get better results

  • Give a post a clear, distinct Internal Title before publishing. It is what shows in the Post Performance table, and a generic or blank title makes it hard to tell posts apart once you're sorting by views.
  • Compare like with like: sort by Views to see reach, then switch to Likes or Comments to see what actually resonated, since the highest-view post is not always the highest-engagement one.
  • If you're trying to judge TikTok or LinkedIn performance, check those numbers directly on the platform for now. This table will not show them until Vidflow adds support.
  • Use the branded report alongside this table when reporting to a client. The report summarizes publishing volume and pipeline; this table is where the platform-level engagement numbers live. See Branded client reports.

#Troubleshooting

  • A published post never shows up here. Confirm it actually went out through Vidflow (not posted directly on the platform), and that it published to X, YouTube, Instagram, or Facebook. TikTok and LinkedIn posts are expected to be absent.
  • Some metrics read 0 while others have real numbers. This is normal on YouTube (no share count) and Facebook (no view count) with today's permissions; it is not a bug.
  • A post has been live for more than a day with no numbers at all. The most common cause is an expired connection on that account. Reconnect it under Workspace Settings > Connections; the next polling pass picks it back up. See Fix publishing problems.
  • Numbers look lower than what you see on the platform itself. Vidflow pulls on its own roughly-6-hour cycle, so there is always some lag versus checking the platform in real time.
