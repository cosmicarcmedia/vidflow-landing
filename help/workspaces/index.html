Workspaces and brand spaces

Updated July 9, 2026

A workspace, also called a brand space, holds one brand's content, clips, and publishing settings. Everything you do in Vidflow happens inside a workspace.

#What a workspace isolates

Each workspace has its own:

  • Episodes, clips, and Content Library.
  • Brand voice and brand sheet.
  • Connected social accounts.
  • Schedules, queues, and the Calendar.
  • Approval links.
  • Notification settings.

Switching to a different workspace switches all of it at once. Nothing from one brand's workspace shows up in another, which is what makes Vidflow safe to use across multiple client brands.

#Your first workspace

Every account includes one workspace, on every plan, at no cost. If you only manage one brand, you likely never need to think about this again. Running content for more than one brand requires a bigger plan; see Running multiple brands for how that works.

#Switching workspaces

Fresh sign-ins land on the Your Brand Spaces screen, showing a card for each workspace you belong to. Returning sessions go straight to the last workspace you were working in. Click any card to switch into that workspace.

#Creating a workspace

  1. From Your Brand Spaces, click to create a new one.
  2. In the Create New Brand Space dialog, enter a Brand Name.
  3. Confirm the auto-suggested URL slug, or adjust it.
  4. Click Create Brand Space.

#Renaming a workspace

Open Workspace Settings and update the workspace name from the General tab. This is an admin-level action.

#Freezing a workspace

Freezing is the reversible way to shut down a workspace you are not ready to permanently delete. From Workspace Settings, Danger Zone, click Freeze. Freezing halts all operations immediately, no new episodes, clips, or posts can be created, and schedules the workspace for permanent deletion in 90 days.

If you change your mind, click Unfreeze on the same tab any time before the 90 days are up to reactivate the workspace exactly as it was.

#Permanently deleting a workspace

Permanent deletion is immediate and irreversible. From Workspace Settings, Danger Zone, start the delete flow and type the workspace's exact name to confirm. This removes the workspace's episodes, clips, posts, schedules, and settings, and cannot be undone.

Important

Important: Use Freeze if there is any chance you will want this workspace back. Permanent delete does not have a recovery window.
