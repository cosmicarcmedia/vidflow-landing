Magic Text Posts

Updated July 10, 2026

Magic Text Posts turns one source, an episode transcript, a saved script, or a plain prompt, into a batch of text posts written in your brand voice. You review the batch, then send everything to your Content Library, a queue, or the calendar in one step.

Find it under Magic Tools in the left rail.

#How it works

Every generation reads your workspace's brand voice settings, your brand sheet, target audience, and voice profile if you have built one from a YouTube channel, and folds them straight into the prompt the AI writes from. That is the difference between a generic post and one that sounds like your brand: the same source material and the same post type will read differently in two workspaces with different brand voice settings. Post type (Quote, Insight, Tip, and so on) shapes the structure, source material supplies the substance, and your brand voice settings supply the actual voice.

#Before you start

  • Set up your brand voice. Until you do, the page shows a banner reading "Your brand voice isn't set up yet" and the AI writes in a generic voice. See Set up your brand voice.
  • To generate from an episode, that episode needs a transcript. Episodes that have one show a green transcript badge in the source picker. See Transcripts.
  • Each generation counts toward your plan's monthly AI allowance. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.

#Generate a batch

  1. Open Magic Text Posts and pick a content source: From Episode / Transcript, From Script, or Free Prompt / Topic.
  2. Leave Platform-agnostic on to get posts written to work on any platform, with no platform-specific hooks or formatting. Turning it off enables per-platform writing on the Pro and Agency plans (details below).
  3. Set a quantity for each post type you want. The Total Posts counter tracks the batch size.
  4. Under After Generating, Add Posts To..., pick a destination: Content Library, Queue, or Schedule.
  5. Click Generate Posts.

Post types

  • Quote: a direct quote or paraphrase from the source.
  • Insight: a key lesson extracted and reframed.
  • Tip: specific, actionable advice.
  • Story: a short narrative moment.
  • Question: a thought-provoking question for engagement.
  • Statistic: a specific number or data point.

#Review and save

Generated posts appear as cards on a review step. Every card starts selected. Deselect any post you do not want to keep, then click the save button at the bottom. Its label confirms the count and destination, for example "Save 8 Posts to Library".

Where the posts land depends on the destination you chose:

  • Content Library: posts are saved as drafts so you can review, edit, and publish them later.
  • Queue: posts join the queue you picked and go out in its upcoming time slots. Pick an existing queue, or choose + Create new queue and give it a name.
  • Schedule: posts are spread across upcoming days at your Posts per day: rate (1, 2, or 3), starting today, each set for 9:00 AM in your local time. They show up on the calendar as scheduled posts.

#Per-platform targeting (Pro and Agency)

On the Pro and Agency plans you can switch Platform-agnostic off and pick target platforms. Vidflow then writes each post with hooks and formatting for its platform, and saves it with that platform pre-selected as the posting target.

The platform choices are X, LinkedIn, and Facebook. Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube are not offered here because they reject text-only posts.

See Plans and features for what your plan includes.

#Get better results

  • Fill in your brand voice before you generate anything real. A filled brand sheet and a specific target audience change the actual sentences the AI writes, not just the topics. See Set up your brand voice, or use the AI Shortcut to fill it from a conversation you already had with your own AI assistant.
  • Be specific in your target audience field, not just your industry. "Busy founders who feel behind on marketing" steers the AI differently than "small business owners."
  • Generate from a transcript over a free prompt when you can. A real episode gives the AI specific quotes, stories, and stats to pull from instead of writing in generalities.
  • Mix post types instead of generating one type at a time. A batch of Quotes, Tips, and Questions from the same source gives you more variety to choose from on the review step.
  • Deselect weak posts on the review step rather than editing them there. It is faster to drop what does not work and regenerate a fresh batch than to hand-edit a post that missed the mark.

#Troubleshooting

  • The Platform-agnostic switch will not turn off. Per-platform targeting is included on the Pro and Agency plans. See Plans and features.
  • Your episode has no transcript badge. Generate its transcript first, then come back. See Transcripts.
  • Generation stops with a monthly limit message. You have used this month's AI allowance. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.
