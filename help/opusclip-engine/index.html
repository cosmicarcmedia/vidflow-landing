Using OpusClip with Vidflow

Updated July 10, 2026

OpusClip is a third-party clip engine you connect to Vidflow with your own API key. Because it fetches source video itself, it works for YouTube-link episodes as well as uploaded files. It is entirely optional, bring your own key: Vidflow's Native engine needs no key and is the default in the Clip Generator.

Each workspace connects its own OpusClip account. Your clips, credits, and brand templates stay on your OpusClip account, not a shared Vidflow key.

#How it works

Vidflow sends your source video and options to OpusClip's API. OpusClip does its own moment selection and rendering on its servers, using whichever brand template and clip mode you chose, then hands the finished clips back through a webhook. Vidflow copies each finished clip into its own storage as soon as it arrives, so it stays reachable in your library even after OpusClip's own links expire. Because OpusClip fetches and downloads the source itself, it can work from a bare YouTube link, unlike Native, which needs an uploaded file.

#Connect your OpusClip API key

  1. Go to Workspace Settings, then the Connections tab.
  2. Find the OpusClip card. If it is not connected, you will see Connect required.
  3. Click Connect OpusClip account.
  4. Paste your OpusClip API key. You can find it in the OpusClip dashboard under Settings > API.
  5. Optionally add an Organization ID (the x-opus-org-id header), needed only for multi-org OpusClip accounts.
  6. Click Test & Save. Vidflow validates the key with a zero-credit call before saving it.

Once connected, the card shows a Connected badge and a masked version of your key. Click Replace key to swap it, or Disconnect to remove it.

Note

Note: OpusClip requires an OpusClip Pro (Beta) or Business plan with API access on your OpusClip account. Credits are billed by OpusClip directly and are metered separately from your Vidflow plan.

If OpusClip is not connected when you try to generate, the Clip Generator disables the Generate Clips button and shows: "OpusClip isn't connected for this workspace yet. Add this workspace's OpusClip API key in Workspace Settings, Connections. Or switch to the Native engine above, no connector needed."

#Options

From the Clip Generator, select the OpusClip tab, then set:

  • Brand template: pick a template from your connected OpusClip account, or leave it on Opus default.
  • Clip mode: ClipBasic (Opus auto-detects the best moments; add genre or keywords to focus it) or ClipAnything (describe exactly what to clip in your own words).
  • Genre and Keywords (ClipBasic only, both optional): keywords are comma-separated.
  • What should Opus clip? (ClipAnything only): a free-text description, for example "Every moment the guest gives concrete, actionable advice."
  • Clip lengths: choose Auto, or one or more of <30s, 30-60s, 60-90s, 90s-3min. Auto is exclusive of the others.
  • Aspect ratio: Portrait 9:16 (default), Landscape 16:9, or Square 1:1.
  • Remove filler words: a switch to strip filler words from the clip.
  • Only clip part of the video: a switch that limits Opus to a specific time range. For uploaded files, you get a scrubbable video preview with Set start to and Set end to buttons. For YouTube-link episodes, enter start and end times manually as mm:ss. The end time must come after the start time.

Click Generate Clips to start the run.

#What happens next

You will see the toast "OpusClip is generating clips, they'll appear in your library shortly." OpusClip clips arrive through a webhook and a background poller checks in periodically as a backup. Finished clips are copied to Vidflow's own storage automatically, so they stay available after OpusClip's temporary links expire.

#Get better results

  • Use ClipAnything when you want a specific kind of moment. A focused prompt like "every moment the guest gives concrete, actionable advice" narrows the search far more than ClipBasic's genre and keyword fields.
  • Add genre or keywords to ClipBasic instead of leaving it fully automatic. Both are optional, but either one gives Opus's own selection a starting point instead of a blind auto-detect pass.
  • Trim to the relevant range on long sources. If only part of a two-hour recording is worth clipping, turn on Only clip part of the video and set the range, so Opus is not searching footage you will never use.
  • Pick a brand template that matches the platform you are posting to, so clips come back close to final instead of needing extra edits in Vidflow afterward.
  • Turn on Remove filler words for cleaner talking-head clips, especially useful for unscripted or conversational sources.

#Troubleshooting

If the OpusClip card will not validate your key, double check you copied the full key from the OpusClip dashboard and that your OpusClip plan includes API access. For stuck or missing clips after generating, see Clips troubleshooting.
