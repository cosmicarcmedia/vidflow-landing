Help / Publishing and scheduling

Publish episodes to YouTube and your podcast feed

Updated July 9, 2026

Full episodes publish to YouTube, and to your podcast feed, from the episode itself rather than from Post now or the Calendar.

#Open the Publishing tab

  1. Open the episode from the Content Library.
  2. Go to its Publishing tab.

You'll see two cards: YouTube and your podcast feed.

#Upload to YouTube

  1. On the YouTube card, choose a privacy setting: Public, Private, or Unlisted. The default is Private.
  2. Start the upload.

The episode's title, description, and tags feed the upload automatically. Once the upload completes, the card shows a Published badge.

Because the default is Private, double-check the privacy dropdown before uploading if you want the video visible right away.

#Publish to your podcast feed

Your workspace has its own podcast RSS feed, independent of YouTube. On the podcast feed card:

  • Publish to Feed adds the episode to your feed.
  • Once added, the card reads Published to feed.
  • If it hasn't been added yet, it reads Not in podcast feed.

Your feed URL follows this pattern:

/api/rss/your-workspace-slug

It's a standard RSS 2.0 feed with iTunes podcast tags, so it works with Spotify for Podcasters, Apple Podcasts, and most other podcast apps and directories. The feed lists your published episodes, most recent first.

Podcast feed settings (like the feed's title and artwork) live in your workspace settings.

#Clips are handled separately

The Publishing tab only uploads the full episode. To publish a clip to Instagram Reels or Facebook, open the clip itself from the Clips tab and use its Publish button. See Post now and Edit clips: captions, reframing, thumbnails.

#Scheduled clips on YouTube

If you schedule a clip through a schedule or queue rather than uploading the full episode manually, that clip publishes to YouTube as a Shorts-eligible public video. It uses the item's title and caption as the video's title and description, with the requested privacy set to public.

This is different from the episode upload above, which respects whatever privacy you choose and defaults to Private.

#YouTube's daily upload quota

YouTube enforces a daily upload quota per connected channel. If a scheduled post hits that quota, Vidflow does not mark it failed. Instead, it defers the post and retries automatically once the daily quota resets, without any action from you. Any other YouTube posts still waiting in that same run are deferred the same way.

#Troubleshooting

The YouTube card won't let you upload: make sure YouTube is connected. See Connect YouTube.

"YouTube authorization was revoked": your Google connection needs to be reconnected. Go to Workspace Settings > Connections and reconnect YouTube.

A scheduled YouTube post seems stuck: check whether it's showing as deferred due to the daily quota. It will retry automatically after the reset; no action is needed.

The podcast feed looks empty or out of date: confirm the episodes you expect are marked Published to feed on their own Publishing tabs. Only published episodes appear in the feed.
