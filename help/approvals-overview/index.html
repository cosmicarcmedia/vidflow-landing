Client approvals overview

Updated July 9, 2026

Approvals let you put finished content in front of a client from a single shareable link. The client reviews on any device, with no Vidflow account and no login.

#Why approval links

If you run content for clients, the review step is usually the slowest part of the pipeline: screenshots pasted into email threads, "any word on last week's posts?" follow-ups, and change requests scattered across three channels.

An approval link replaces that. You pick the content, Vidflow generates one URL, and everything the client needs lives behind it. The page carries your logo, your workspace name, and your brand color. Vidflow's branding does not appear, so to your client it reads as your review portal.

#How it works

  1. Create a link from the Approvals page. Give it a title, pick the content, and set an optional expiry between 1 and 90 days.
  2. Send the URL to your client through whatever channel you already use.
  3. The client opens it in a browser and approves items or leaves comments. There is no reject button; change requests come back as comments.
  4. Every approval and comment saves the moment it happens. There is no submit step for the client to forget.
  5. You check progress from the Approvals page. Vidflow does not send you a notification when a client approves or comments, so build the check into your routine.

Once an item is approved, it is cleared to publish. Scheduled posts that are still waiting on approval are held back, then go out after approval.

#Where approvals plug in

  • Schedules: every schedule has a Require Approval toggle, so content must be approved before it posts through that schedule. See Schedules and queues.
  • Auto-import: imported content can arrive marked as needing approval, so nothing a client drops in a folder publishes unseen. See Auto-import from Google Drive or Dropbox.
  • Flows: the Approvals node routes generated content into review as part of an automated pipeline. See Flow nodes reference.

#Availability

Approvals are available on every paid plan, from Starter up. See Plans and features. Your reviewers never need an account or a seat; anyone with the link can review.
