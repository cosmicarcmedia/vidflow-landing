Your first content pack

Updated July 10, 2026

Right after you finish or skip the onboarding wizard, Vidflow asks for one thing: your first episode. Hand it a YouTube link or a video file and it builds a full batch of content from it automatically, no need to open the Clip Generator or Magic Text Posts yourself for this first run.

#How it works

Submitting your first episode kicks off Vidflow's default content flow against it: the episode is transcribed, then the AI writes text posts, designs images, and writes captions, all in the brand voice and image style you set up during onboarding, tags everything, and queues it to your calendar. If you uploaded a video file, native clip generation runs too, cutting short clips straight from the footage. You do not have to trigger any of this by hand, it starts the moment the episode is submitted and you can watch it happen on a live progress screen.

#Add your first episode

  1. On the Add your first episode screen, choose Paste a YouTube link or Upload a file.
  2. For a YouTube link, paste the URL and click Add.
  3. For a file, click Choose video or audio file and pick a video or audio file from your computer.
  4. Vidflow starts working immediately and switches to the progress screen.

You can also click Skip for now below the card to go straight to your Dashboard and add an episode later from the Make Content menu instead.

#What you get depends on how you added it

This is the one honest tradeoff worth knowing up front:

  • Uploaded file: the full pack, posts, images, captions, and native clips cut straight from your video.
  • Pasted YouTube link: posts, images, and captions from the transcript, but no clips. Native clipping needs the actual video file, which a YouTube import never downloads. If you want clips from a video you linked, upload the file as well and Vidflow adds clipping to the run.

#The live progress screen

While your pack builds, a stage list shows each step as it happens: transcribing your episode, writing posts in your voice, designing images in your style, writing captions, tagging, adding to your calendar, and, for an uploaded file, cutting clips. Each stage shows pending, in progress, done, or skipped, with a percentage bar tracking overall progress. You can leave this page any time, the pack keeps building in the background regardless of whether you're watching.

When it finishes, you land on a summary of what was made, how many posts, images, and clips, with See your content pack taking you to the Content Library and See your calendar taking you to the Calendar. If the run produced nothing (rare, but possible on a very short or unusual source), you'll see a note that the episode itself is still saved and ready to work with even though nothing generated.

If something goes wrong partway through, the screen tells you plainly and offers Try again or Add a different episode, nothing you already submitted is lost either way.

#Where results land

Everything the first content pack generates, posts, images, clips, and the episode itself, lands in your Content Library just like anything generated later from the Clip Generator or Magic Text Posts. Generated posts and clips are already tagged and queued onto your Calendar rather than sitting unscheduled, so this first run leaves you with an actual week of content ready to review, not just raw material.

#Get better results

  • Upload the video file, not just a YouTube link, if you want native clips as part of this first run.
  • Finish the onboarding wizard's voice and audience steps before submitting your episode, the more Vidflow knows about your brand voice going in, the less generic the first batch of posts sounds.
  • Use a source with clean, single-speaker audio for the best transcript and, if you uploaded a file, the strongest clip selection.
  • If the pack comes back thin or off-voice, that's a signal to go fill in your brand voice guidelines and target audience in Workspace Settings, then generate a fresh batch from the Clip Generator or Magic Text Posts, both read the same brand settings this first run does.

#Troubleshooting

I only got posts and images, no clips. You added your episode from a YouTube link. Clips need an uploaded video file. Upload the file to the same episode, or add it again through Upload Episode, and clip generation will run.

The progress screen has been on the same stage for a while. Transcription and generation both take a few minutes depending on video length. Leave the tab open or come back later, the run continues in the background either way.

It finished but nothing was generated. Some sources produce nothing usable on a first pass, most often a very short or low-content video. The episode itself is still saved. Open it from the Content Library and try the Clip Generator or Magic Text Posts on it manually.
