Signing in and resetting your password

Updated July 10, 2026

Vidflow supports two sign-in methods, and a self-serve password reset if you forget yours.

#How it works

Sign-in is checked against your invite before anything else, whichever method you use. If two-factor authentication is on for your account, it's an extra step after your password or Google login is accepted, not a replacement for either. A password reset always confirms it went through, whether or not that email actually has a Vidflow account, so the confirmation message itself never leaks whether an email is registered.

#Sign in with Google

  1. Go to app.vidflow.io.
  2. Click Continue with Google.
  3. Choose the Google account tied to your Vidflow invite.

Your email address must be verified with Google and must match an email that has been granted Vidflow access. See Getting access to Vidflow if you have not been invited yet.

#Sign in with email and password

  1. Go to app.vidflow.io.
  2. Enter your email and password.
  3. Click Sign in.

If your account has two-factor authentication turned on, you are prompted for a second step after your password is accepted. See Account security for how to set up and manage two-factor authentication.

#Resetting your password

  1. On the sign-in screen, click Forgot password?.
  2. Enter the email address you sign in with and submit the form.
  3. Check your inbox for an email with the subject "Reset your Vidflow password." For your privacy, this step always confirms the request went through, whether or not that email has a Vidflow account.
  4. Click the reset link in the email. It opens Vidflow directly on the Set a new password screen.
  5. Enter a new password and submit.
Note

Note: The reset link expires one hour after it is sent and can only be used once.

Important

Important: Setting a new password signs you out everywhere else, on every device and browser where you were previously signed in. You will need to sign back in with the new password on each of them.

#Get better results

  • Sign in with Google if your invite email is a Google account, it's one click and skips password management entirely.
  • Turn on two-factor authentication once you're in, especially if your workspace holds client accounts or approval links. See Account security.
  • Use the reset link the moment it arrives. It expires after one hour and works only once, so a stale one just means requesting a fresh one.

#Troubleshooting

The reset link says it is invalid or expired. Reset links expire after one hour and can only be used once. Request a new one from the Forgot password? link.

I did not get the reset email. Check spam. If it still has not arrived after a few minutes, submit the request again. The confirmation message you see after submitting does not guarantee delivery, only that the request was received.

I am asked for a code after my password is accepted. That is two-factor authentication on your account. Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app, or use a recovery code if you have lost access to the app. Details on setting up, disabling, or regenerating recovery codes are in Account security.

My account got locked out after several failed attempts. Vidflow temporarily locks sign-in attempts after repeated failures from the same email and location, as a brute-force protection. Wait a while and try again, or use the password reset flow instead of guessing.
