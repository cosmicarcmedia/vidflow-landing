AI images and thumbnails

Updated July 10, 2026

Vidflow generates images where you already work: an Image Studio inside the post editor, a standalone generator in the Content Library, AI thumbnails in the clip editor, and a light image editor for crops and filters. Every image generation counts toward your plan's monthly AI allowance. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.

#How it works

Your prompt supplies the subject, and your workspace's saved image style, set in Brand Settings or during the onboarding wizard, supplies the look: color palette, composition, mood, whatever you described when you set it up. When a style is set, it is applied before any other style choice the AI would otherwise make on its own, so the same prompt produces a consistent look across every image you generate instead of a different style each time. With AI Quality Pass turned on, a vision model checks each result against your brand rubric and regenerates automatically until it passes, up to three attempts, before handing you the result.

#Image Studio

Image Studio generates and refines images for a text or image post.

  1. Open a text or image post from your Content Library in the edit view.
  2. Click Image Studio. The studio opens beside the post; Close Studio hides it again.
  3. Describe the image in the prompt box, or click Magic prompt to draft a prompt from the post's title and caption.
  4. Pick an Aspect Ratio: 3:4, 4:5, 1:1, 9:16, 16:9, or 1.91:1. Each option notes the placements it suits, such as feed, story, or link preview.
  5. Optional: pick a Social style preset (curated art direction), or a Style template from your brand's saved Image Presets. With a template, the template controls the look and your prompt supplies the subject.
  6. Click Generate image.

Every result lands in a strip of generations beside the preview, so you can compare attempts and return to an earlier one. From there:

  • With an image selected, the main button becomes Refine this image: describe the change and Vidflow edits that image. Regenerate from scratch starts over from the prompt alone.
  • Upload image adds your own file to the strip, so you can refine an existing graphic or use it as-is.
  • Hover a thumbnail in the strip to view or edit the prompt that produced it.
  • Click Select this image to attach the selected image to the post.

#Generate a standalone image

To create an image that is not attached to an existing post, click Generate Image at the top of your Content Library.

  1. Describe the image in the prompt box.
  2. Pick an Aspect Ratio: 1:1, 4:5, 3:4, or 1.91:1.
  3. Click Generate Image. Use Regenerate for another take on the same prompt.
  4. Optional: give the image a title. Without one, Vidflow titles it from the prompt.
  5. Click Save to Library. The image lands in your library as a draft image post, ready to schedule or to refine further in Image Studio.

#Clip thumbnails

When you edit a clip, thumbnail tools sit under the video player. Pause the video on the frame you want first.

  • Use this frame as thumbnail captures the paused frame as the clip's thumbnail.
  • AI thumbnail for Shorts generates a branded Shorts thumbnail from the current frame.

For the rest of the clip editor, see Edit clips: captions, reframing, thumbnails.

#Edit an image

When a post has an image, an Edit image (crop, rotate, filters) link appears under the preview in the edit view. The Edit image dialog covers:

  • Rotate and flip controls.
  • Crop aspect: Original, 1:1, 4:5, 9:16, or 16:9, with a Position slider to place the crop.
  • Brightness and Saturation sliders, plus a Grayscale toggle.

Click Apply & save to save the edited image, or Reset all to discard your adjustments.

#Carousel images (Pro and Agency)

On image posts, the Carousel Images section turns one post into a multi-image carousel of up to 10 images. Click Add Images to upload more, add the post's current image to the set, and reorder the images. Carousel building is included on the Pro and Agency plans. See Plans and features.

#Image Presets in Brand Settings (Pro and Agency)

Brand Settings includes an Image Presets card where you save named, reusable image prompts (a name plus the prompt itself), up to 20 per brand space, for looks you use again and again, like quote cards or infographics. Saved presets appear as Style template options in Image Studio and in image generation inside Flows.

The same card sets the default image model for the brand space and offers an AI Quality Pass switch: after each generation, a vision model checks the image against your brand rubric and regenerates until it passes, spending up to three generations and three reviews on a single image.

Managing presets and these settings is included on the Pro and Agency plans. See Plans and features.

You can also set a starting image style during onboarding. See The onboarding wizard.

#Get better results

  • Save an image style preset before you generate anything you plan to keep. A saved style is the single biggest lever on how on-brand a generated image looks, far more than wording the prompt more carefully. Set it in Brand Settings > Image Presets or during the onboarding wizard. See The onboarding wizard.
  • Let a template control the look and your prompt control the subject. With a Style template picked, describe only what should be in the image, colors, composition, and mood are already handled.
  • Refine instead of regenerating from scratch when a result is close. Refine this image keeps what is working and changes only what you describe; a full regenerate throws away a result that may have been one tweak away.
  • Turn on AI Quality Pass for anything client-facing. It costs extra generations behind the scenes but catches results that miss your brand rubric before you ever see them.
  • Use Magic prompt as a starting point, not a finished prompt. It drafts from the post's title and caption, then add specifics (setting, mood, what should and should not appear) before generating.

#Troubleshooting

  • Generate image stays disabled. Enter a prompt first, or click Magic prompt to draft one.
  • Generation fails with an error. Run it again. If it keeps failing, check whether you have reached your monthly AI allowance. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.
  • Carousel or preset controls are locked. Both are included on the Pro and Agency plans. See Plans and features.
