Help / Troubleshooting and FAQ

Fix publishing problems

Updated July 10, 2026

When a post fails, work through this checklist in order. It is ordered by how often each cause shows up, so start at the top.

#How it works

Every scheduled or queued post is picked up by a background dispatcher that publishes to each of its target platforms independently, records a per-platform result, and marks the item's overall status from those results. That is why one post can show as published while carrying a specific error for just one platform: each platform call succeeds or fails on its own.

#1. Check if the account's authorization died

The most common cause of a failed post is a connected account whose authorization stopped working on the platform's end. When this happens, Vidflow pauses publishing to that account, emails the workspace, and marks it as needing reconnect. The email reads:

"Your {platform} connection stopped working, so we've paused publishing to it. This usually happens when {platform} expires the authorization on their end, and it takes about a minute to fix. Reconnect the account and your scheduled posts will pick right back up."

Fix it:

  1. Go to Workspace Settings > Connections.
  2. Find the account with a needs-reconnect status.
  3. Reconnect it by signing in again.

Once reconnected, anything waiting on that account resumes normally.

Note

Note: Reconnect timing varies by platform. YouTube and TikTok refresh tokens automatically in the background and only need reconnect when the platform itself revokes access. Meta (Instagram and Facebook) tokens are longer-lived but do not refresh automatically once they expire, so a dead Meta connection needs a manual reconnect. LinkedIn tokens last around 60 days, so expect to reconnect it periodically even without an error.

#2. Check the platform's requirements

Some platforms reject posts that do not meet their format requirements, and Vidflow catches most of these before the post goes out.

  • Video is required on TikTok and YouTube. A TikTok post with no video attached fails with a message naming the item's type and that TikTok needs video; YouTube behaves the same way, since both platforms only accept video uploads through this integration. Attach a video to the item, or post it to a platform that also accepts text or images.
  • Media (image or video) is required on Instagram. A text-only item fails on Instagram specifically. Facebook and X both accept text-only posts.
  • Caption or title is over the platform's limit. Each platform enforces its own cap: X 280 characters, Instagram and TikTok 2200, LinkedIn 3000, YouTube 5000, and Facebook up to roughly 63,000. The post editor shows a live character counter per platform so you can catch this before it fails.
  • TikTok compose settings are incomplete or conflict with each other. TikTok requires you to set who can view the post before it goes out. Common validation messages:
    • "Choose who can view your TikTok post before scheduling it." (no audience selected yet)
    • "TikTok disclosure is on: select Your brand or Branded content, or turn disclosure off." (disclosure toggled on with no content type chosen)
    • "Branded content on TikTok cannot be private. Choose a different audience." (branded content and a private audience were selected together; branded content can never be private)

Fix any of these in the post's TikTok settings panel before retrying.

#3. Check for a YouTube quota hold

If YouTube posts are stuck in scheduled or publishing status, this is usually not a failure. YouTube enforces a daily upload quota, and when Vidflow hits it, the post is automatically deferred, not marked failed. Internally this shows as "YouTube daily quota reached; will retry automatically after quota reset."

What to do: nothing. Vidflow reschedules the item for shortly after the next quota reset (YouTube's quota resets at midnight Pacific time) and retries automatically. If it still has not gone out a full day later, contact support.

Beta note

Beta note: Separately from quota, a YouTube upload that succeeds still publishes as Private rather than your chosen visibility, until Vidflow's YouTube integration completes Google's app verification. This is not a failure and needs no retry; change the video's visibility directly in YouTube Studio once you've reviewed it.

#4. Check for an old X connection missing media permission

If a post with an image or video fails on X but text-only posts to the same account work fine, the X connection was likely made before Vidflow requested the media upload permission. Older connections do not have it and cannot post media until reconnected.

Fix it: go to Workspace Settings > Connections and reconnect the X account. This grants the missing permission without losing anything else about the connection.

#5. Check if approval is holding it back

Content that needs approval is never picked up for publishing, whether it is on a schedule with Require Approval turned on or was imported with approval required. This is expected behavior, not a failure. The item sits in a needs-approval state until someone approves it, either from your approval workflow or directly in the Content Library.

Check the item's approval badge in the Content Library. If it reads Needs Approval, that is why it has not gone out. See Approval status in your library.

#6. Retry the post

Once you have fixed the underlying cause, retry the failed post rather than recreating it:

  • From the Content Library, open the item's menu and click Retry Post.
  • From the Calendar, open the failed post and click Retry Post.

Retrying puts the post back in the queue to publish again with the fix applied.

#7. Still failing? Contact support

If you have checked authorization, platform requirements, YouTube quota, and approval status, and the post still fails, copy the exact failure text shown on the post and send it to support. The failure text names which platform failed and why, and that detail is the fastest way for support to diagnose it. See Contact support.

Note

Note: A single post can target multiple platforms at once. If some platforms succeeded and others did not, the post shows as published overall, with the specific per-platform errors still visible on the item so you can see exactly which platform needs attention.

#Get better results

  • Reconnect accounts proactively rather than waiting for a failure email, especially LinkedIn, which expires on its own roughly every 60 days even when nothing looks wrong.
  • Attach video to anything you plan to send to TikTok or YouTube from the start. Routing a text-only or image-only item to either platform will always fail, no matter how many times you retry it.
  • Check the character counter in the editor before you save, not after a failed post tells you. It is the exact same limit the dispatcher enforces, so a green counter means that platform will not reject it on length.
  • When a multi-platform post partially fails, use Retry Post rather than deleting and recreating it. Retry only re-attempts the platforms that failed, so successful platforms are not touched again.
