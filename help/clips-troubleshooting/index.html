Clips troubleshooting

Updated July 10, 2026

Common problems when generating or editing clips, and what to do about each one.

#"OpusClip isn't connected for this workspace yet"

This appears in the Clip Generator when you select the OpusClip engine but no OpusClip API key is connected for the current workspace. The Generate Clips button is disabled until you fix this.

Fix it one of two ways:

  1. Go to Workspace Settings > Connections and connect this workspace's OpusClip API key. See Using OpusClip with Vidflow.
  2. Or switch to the Native engine, which needs no connector.

#"Native clips need the actual video file"

This appears when you try to run the Native engine on an episode that was added as a link (for example, a YouTube URL) with no uploaded media. Native cannot work from a link alone.

Fix it one of two ways:

  1. Upload the video file to that episode, then run Native again.
  2. Or switch to the OpusClip engine, which can fetch link-only sources itself.

#Generation seems stuck

After you click Generate Clips, the status card reads "Generating your clips." with "This usually takes a few minutes." Most runs finish within that window.

Note

Note: If clips have not appeared in your Content Library after roughly 30 minutes, re-run the generation, or contact support if it happens again. Do not assume a failed run will always show an error message on screen.

Before re-running, check the Clips tab in your Content Library directly. Clips are added there automatically as soon as they finish rendering, and this is more reliable than watching the generation status card.

#You have reached your monthly clip allowance

Clip generations draw from your workspace's shared monthly allowance. If you hit the limit, Vidflow blocks further generations until the next calendar month or until you upgrade. See Usage limits and monthly allowances for how the allowance works and Plans and features for what each plan includes.

#Where finished clips appear

Every finished clip lands in your Content Library automatically, whether it came from Native or OpusClip. Open the Clips tab (or click View clips from the Clip Generator's status card) to find them. Clips are tagged by source so you can tell Native and OpusClip clips apart.

#Captions are hard to read against the footage

This usually means the caption style is fighting the background rather than something being broken. Plain white text can wash out against light or busy footage. Open the clip, switch to Box Background in the Captions tab or the AI Clip Writing panel, and click Render Edited Clip to burn in the new style. See Caption styles for what each style looks like and when to reach for it.

#A rendered clip has a watermark and end card

Free and trial workspaces get a Vidflow watermark and end card on rendered clips, plus a #vidflow hashtag on trial captions. This is expected, not a bug. It goes away on a paid plan; Starter keeps only the light end card. See Plans and features.
