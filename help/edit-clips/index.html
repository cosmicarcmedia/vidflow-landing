Edit clips: captions, reframing, thumbnails

Updated July 10, 2026

Once a clip lands in your Content Library, open it to rewrite the title and captions in your brand voice, adjust how it is reframed and captioned, pick a new thumbnail, or swap the underlying video.

#How it works

Your edits in the clip detail dialog, reframing, caption style, on-screen hook text, are saved as instructions rather than applied instantly. Clicking Render Edited Clip is what actually burns those instructions into a fresh MP4, running the same rendering module clips go through when they are first generated. The original clip file stays untouched until you render, so you can try a different crop or caption style and re-render as many times as you want without losing your starting point.

#Open a clip

From the Content Library, open a clip item to reach the clip detail dialog. It has three tabs: Details, Reframe, and Captions.

  • Details: review the clip, approve it, share it, and render edited exports.
  • Reframe: adjust the crop used to reframe the clip, or turn on a gentle pan/zoom.
  • Captions: edit caption timing.

#Adjust the reframing

On the Reframe tab:

  • Crop: drag the Left Offset, Top Offset, Width, and Height sliders to move and resize the crop window against a preview of the clip.
  • Pan/Zoom: turn on Enable gentle pan/zoom and set a Duration to add a slow pan across the frame instead of a static crop. This is saved as metadata and applied the next time the clip is rendered or exported.

Click Save Reframing to keep your changes.

#AI Clip Writing

The AI Clip Writing panel generates titles and captions from the clip's transcript, using your workspace's voice profile.

  1. Choose a Caption Style.
  2. Click Generate Title for a title, or generate captions per platform, or click Generate All Platform Captions to do every connected platform at once.
  3. The panel shows badges for the active style ("Style: {style}") and confirms "Voice profile active" when your brand voice is applied.

#Render your edits

From the Details tab, under Rendered Export:

  1. Make your reframing and caption edits in the other tabs.
  2. Optionally check Add on-screen hook text during render and enter the text to burn in.
  3. Click Render Edited Clip. This burns your caption and reframing edits into a fresh MP4.
  4. Once rendering finishes, click Open Rendered MP4 to view it.

#Thumbnails

From the clip's media tools, you can:

  • Use this frame as thumbnail: capture the current video frame as the clip's thumbnail.
  • AI thumbnail for Shorts: generate a branded, high-CTR Shorts thumbnail from the current frame.

#Replace the video file

Click Replace content to swap the underlying video file on a clip. Title, captions, tags, and schedule are all kept. If the replacement file is a different length than the original, Vidflow shows a confirmation: "These clips aren't the same length."

#Round trip to OpusClip

If the clip came from OpusClip, click Edit in OpusClip to open the clip's project directly in OpusClip. Make your changes there, export, then come back to Vidflow and use Replace content to bring in the updated export.

#Get better results

  • Re-render instead of tweaking blindly. A crop or caption-style change only shows up in the actual video after you click Render Edited Clip. Check the rendered MP4 before deciding it needs another pass.
  • Try a different caption style from the library rather than fighting one style's placement. Box Background is built for footage where a plain style is hard to read. See Caption styles.
  • Use pan/zoom sparingly. A short, gentle pan reads as intentional motion; a long or fast one reads as shaky. Keep the duration modest for a talking-head crop.
  • Pause on a clean frame before generating a thumbnail. Both Use this frame as thumbnail and AI thumbnail for Shorts work from whatever frame the player is paused on, so pick a frame with a clear, expressive shot of the speaker first.

#Troubleshooting

If a render gets stuck or a replaced clip will not save, see Clips troubleshooting. For platform caption length limits when you post a clip, see Post now.
