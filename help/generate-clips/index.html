Generate clips from a long video

Updated July 9, 2026

Use the Clip Generator to turn a long-form video into a batch of short clips. Pick a source, choose an engine, set your options, and Vidflow finds the moments and renders the clips for you.

#Before you start

You need a source video: an existing episode with a video file, an uploaded video or audio file, or a YouTube link. You also need at least one clip engine available. Vidflow ships two:

  • Native, Vidflow's in-house engine. No setup, works out of the box.
  • OpusClip, a third-party engine that requires your own OpusClip API key connected for the workspace.

See The Native clip engine and Using OpusClip with Vidflow for what each one offers.

#Open the Clip Generator

  1. From the app rail, click Clip Generator.
  2. Or, from an episode's detail page, click Generate Clips to open the Clip Generator with that episode preselected.
  3. Or, from a Content Library card's menu, choose Generate Clips.

#Pick a source

Under Source, choose one of three tabs:

  1. Existing episode: select any episode in your library that already has a video file.
  2. Upload file: click Choose video or audio file to upload a new file. This adds the file to your library as a new episode, then clips it.
  3. YouTube link: paste a YouTube URL and click Add. This adds the video to your library as a new episode, then clips it.
Note

Note: A YouTube link alone does not give you a video file to work with. If you plan to use the Native engine, upload the file directly, or import it as an episode with the video attached instead of a bare link.

#Choose an engine

At the top of the page, pick Native or OpusClip. Native is the default engine and is currently in beta.

If OpusClip is not connected for your workspace, you will see a message telling you to add your OpusClip API key in Workspace Settings, Connections, or switch to the Native engine, which needs no connector.

#Set your options and generate

Each engine has its own options panel, covered in The Native clip engine and Using OpusClip with Vidflow. Once your source and options are set, click Generate Clips.

#What happens next

You will see a status card that reads "Generating your clips." with the note "This usually takes a few minutes. Clips land in your Content Library automatically." Click View clips to jump straight to the Clips tab in your Content Library, or navigate there any time from the app rail.

A generation job also appears in your notifications, so you can keep working while it runs.

#Troubleshooting

If clips have not appeared after roughly 30 minutes, re-run the generation or contact support. See Clips troubleshooting for the full list of common issues, including the OpusClip-not-connected warning and the native file-required error.
