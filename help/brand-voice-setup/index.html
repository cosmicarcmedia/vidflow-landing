Set up your brand voice

Updated July 9, 2026

Everything Vidflow generates, clip titles, captions, posts, scripts, draws on the brand voice settings in your workspace. Filling these in is the one setup step that changes how everything else sounds.

#Why this matters

Without a brand sheet, target audience, or voice guidance, the AI has nothing to work from and writes in a generic voice. With them filled in, clip titles, captions, and posts start sounding like your brand instead of a template.

#Your Brand Profile

Open Workspace Settings and go to the Brand tab. The Brand Profile card includes:

  • Brand Name (required).
  • Website, Industry, Location, and Timezone.
  • Target Audience, a free-text field for describing your ideal listener: who they are, what they struggle with, what they want.
  • Campaign Focus Areas, a set of topic chips you add yourself (for example, Leadership or Entrepreneurship) to steer what the AI treats as on-topic.

Below that is the Brand Sheet card, where you paste or type a longer brand document, or upload one. This is the single biggest input into how on-brand your generated content sounds, treat it as the one field worth spending real time on.

#Your logo

The logo you upload with your Brand Profile does more than decorate your own workspace. It also white-labels the client approval portal your clients see when reviewing content, and it appears on your branded analytics reports. Upload a real logo here if you want client-facing surfaces to carry your brand instead of looking generic.

#Teaching the AI your voice

The AI & Voice tab holds the settings that shape tone and style directly:

  • Voice Profile Status shows where you stand: no active profile yet, connect a YouTube account or import a channel to unlock it, or a built profile summarized as built from a number of videos. Voice profiles are part of the Pro and Agency plan set; see Plans and features.
  • Brand voice guidelines is a free-text field for extra writing direction: phrases to lean into, words to avoid, sentence rhythm, anything a style guide would normally cover.
  • Preview my voice generates a short sample so you can check how the current settings sound before you generate real content with them.
  • Humanize AI Writing, with a Remove AI tropes toggle, strips common AI writing patterns from generated text.

#Matching your visual style

The AI & Voice tab also includes a place to describe your visual style, so generated images and graphics match your brand look instead of a generic default. Fill this in once and every generated image draws on it.

#The setup nudge

If your brand sheet, audience, or voice guidelines are missing, Vidflow shows a banner across the Magic Tools pages: "Your brand voice isn't set up yet. Until it is, the AI writes in a generic voice. Add your brand sheet, audience, and voice guidelines so everything sounds like you." Clicking Set up voice on that banner jumps you straight to the Brand Sheet field. The banner disappears once the basics are filled in.
