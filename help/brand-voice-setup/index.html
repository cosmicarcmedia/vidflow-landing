Set up your brand voice

Updated July 10, 2026

Everything Vidflow generates, clip titles, captions, posts, scripts, draws on the brand voice settings in your workspace. Filling these in is the one setup step that changes how everything else sounds. Reach for this article any time generated content feels generic, before your first real batch of content, or whenever your brand's tone or audience shifts.

#How it works

Vidflow does not have a hardcoded writing style. Every AI generation call reads your workspace's brand fields (name, industry, target audience, brand sheet, voice guidelines, voice profile) and injects them into the prompt as context before it writes anything. An empty field is simply missing context, not a smarter default, so the AI falls back to a generic assistant voice. The more specific and current these fields are, the closer every clip title, caption, post, and script lands to how you actually sound.

#Your Brand Profile

Open Workspace Settings and go to the Brand tab. The Brand Profile card includes:

  • Brand Name (required).
  • Website, Industry, Location, and Timezone.
  • Target Audience, a free-text field for describing your ideal listener: who they are, what they struggle with, what they want.
  • Campaign Focus Areas, a set of topic chips you add yourself (for example, Leadership or Entrepreneurship) to steer what the AI treats as on-topic.

Below that is the Brand Sheet card, where you paste or type a longer brand document, or upload one. This is the single biggest input into how on-brand your generated content sounds, treat it as the one field worth spending real time on.

#Your logo

The logo you upload with your Brand Profile does more than decorate your own workspace. It also white-labels the client approval portal your clients see when reviewing content, and it appears on your branded analytics reports. Upload a real logo here if you want client-facing surfaces to carry your brand instead of looking generic.

#Teaching the AI your voice

The AI & Voice tab holds the settings that shape tone and style directly:

  • Voice Profile Status shows where you stand: no active profile yet, connect a YouTube account or import a channel to unlock it, or a built profile summarized as built from a number of videos, with the date it was last built.
  • AI Voice Profile card: click Build Voice Profile to have Vidflow read up to 20 to 30 of your recent YouTube videos and learn your tone, vocabulary, sentence structure, common phrases, and topics. No YouTube channel, or want to skip it? Paste 3 to 5 real captions, posts, or a transcript into the sample-text box instead (50 characters minimum) and click Build from samples. Once built, click Rebuild any time to refresh it, or Clear to remove it.
  • Brand voice guidelines is a free-text field for extra writing direction: phrases to lean into, words to avoid, sentence rhythm, anything a style guide would normally cover.
  • Preview my voice generates a short sample so you can check how the current settings sound before you generate real content with them.
  • Humanize AI Writing, with a Remove AI tropes toggle (on by default), strips em dashes and AI clichés like "dive in" and "unlock" from all generated writing.

#Matching your visual style

The Master Image Style card on the AI & Voice tab holds a style prompt describing your visual look: lighting, color palette, photography or illustration style. It is automatically applied to all workspace image generation, including YouTube thumbnails. Fill it in once, click Test to generate a sample image and confirm it looks right, then every generated image draws on it going forward. This card is a premium feature; see Plans and features.

#The setup nudge

If your brand sheet, audience, or voice guidelines are missing, Vidflow shows a banner across the AI generator pages: "Your brand voice isn't set up yet. Until it is, the AI writes in a generic voice. Add your brand sheet, audience, and voice guidelines so everything sounds like you." Clicking Set up voice on that banner jumps you straight to the Brand Sheet field. The banner disappears once the basics are filled in, and you can dismiss it for the session either way.

#Get better results

  • Import your YouTube channel first. On the AI & Voice tab, connecting or importing a YouTube channel unlocks one-click voice-profile building from your own back catalog, the fastest way to get a voice profile that actually sounds like you instead of a description of you.
  • Use the AI Shortcut if you do not want to type it all out. The AI Shortcut hands a ready-made prompt to ChatGPT, Claude, or whatever assistant you already talk to about your business, and turns its answer into a filled brand profile you can review before applying.
  • Keep Target Audience specific, not broad. "People who like business content" gives the AI nothing to grab onto. "Agency owners with 5 to 20 employees who are drowning in client delivery and want to raise prices without losing clients" tells it exactly who to write for and what problem to speak to.
  • Treat the Brand Sheet as worth real time, not a placeholder. It is the single biggest lever on output quality. A few paragraphs on your positioning, tone, vocabulary to use and avoid, and a couple of real examples of your writing outperforms a one-line description every time.
  • Revisit it as your content evolves. A voice profile built from six-month-old videos, or a brand sheet written before you changed your offer or audience, quietly drags every generation back toward your old voice. Rebuild the voice profile and refresh the brand sheet whenever your content or positioning shifts.
  • Use Preview my voice before a big batch. It costs one AI generation and catches a stale or off-target voice before it shows up across a week of scheduled posts instead of after.
