Help / Connecting social accounts

Connect Instagram and Facebook

Updated July 9, 2026

Instagram and Facebook connect through a single Meta sign-in. One connection can link both platforms at once, since Vidflow discovers your Facebook Page and any Instagram professional account attached to it during the same flow.

#What you need before connecting

  • A Facebook Page. Vidflow publishes to Facebook through a Page, not a personal profile.
  • For Instagram, a professional (business) Instagram account linked to that Facebook Page. A personal Instagram account with no Page connection will not appear.

If your Instagram is not yet set up as a professional account linked to a Page, do that in Meta's own settings first, then connect in Vidflow.

#Connect steps

  1. Open Workspace Settings.
  2. Click the Connections tab.
  3. Click Facebook & Instagram.
  4. Sign in on Meta's page and approve access, including access to your Pages.
  5. You are returned to Vidflow. Vidflow links your Facebook Page automatically, and links Instagram too if a professional Instagram account is attached to that Page.

#What Vidflow can publish

  • Instagram: video posts published as Reels, with a caption.
  • Facebook: video posts to your Page, with a title and description.

Both need a video file. Vidflow does not publish image-only or text-only posts to either platform today.

#Limits enforced

  • Instagram caption: 2200 characters.
  • Facebook title: 255 characters.
  • Facebook description: 5000 characters.
  • A video file is required for both platforms.

#Token lifetime and reconnect

Meta issues a long-lived token when you connect, and Vidflow does not refresh it in the background. When Meta eventually invalidates that token, whether from a password change, a permissions review, or normal expiry, publishing to that account fails and Vidflow pauses it rather than retrying indefinitely. You will get an email explaining that your connection stopped working and that reconnecting will resume anything waiting on your schedule.

#Troubleshooting

No connected account error when trying to post. You will see "No {platform} account connected. Connect one in Brand Settings." This usually means the Meta sign-in did not find a Facebook Page, or found a Page without an attached Instagram professional account. Reconnect and confirm you are approving access to the correct Page.

Facebook Page ID not found. This means the stored connection lost its Page reference. Reconnect your Facebook account from the Connections tab.

Instagram is missing even though Facebook connected. Check that your Instagram account is a professional (business) account and is linked to the same Facebook Page in Meta's settings, then reconnect.

Posts are failing after previously working. Reconnect the account. Meta connections do not self-refresh, so this is the expected fix once Meta invalidates the stored token.
