Episodes vs. library content

Updated July 10, 2026

Vidflow separates what you make content from and what you publish. Episodes are your long-form sources. Library content is the individual pieces that go out to your channels.

#How it works

An episode never publishes on its own, it's raw material. Every clip, post, image, or article that does publish is generated by reading an episode's transcript (or, for library content with no linked episode, standing alone with less context). Keeping this split means the same episode can feed dozens of pieces of content over time without you re-uploading or re-explaining it each time.

#Episodes: your source material

An episode is a long-form source: a video or audio file you upload, or a video imported from YouTube. The episode carries the transcript, show notes, and metadata that the AI works from when it writes clips, captions, and posts.

You create episodes by:

  • Uploading a file. See Upload a video or audio file.
  • Importing from YouTube, either from a connected channel or by pasting any YouTube links. See Import from YouTube.
  • Handing the Clip Generator a file or YouTube link. It saves the video to your library as a new episode, then clips it. See Generate clips from a long video.
  • Adding your very first episode on the onboarding first-episode screen, which also kicks off a starter content pack automatically. See Your first content pack.

Each episode counts toward your plan's monthly episode allowance. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.

#Library content: what you publish

The Content Library holds the pieces you schedule and post: text posts, clips, images, carousels, and articles. These come from the Clip Generator, Magic Text Posts and the other Magic Tools, bulk upload, CSV import, auto-import sources, or manual creation with Create Content.

#Where each shows up

Episodes and content items live side by side in the Content Library, with views that filter to episodes or clips when you want one kind at a time. Opening an episode gives you four tabs: Overview (stats, description, and show notes when present), Clips, Transcript, and Publishing.

#Link content to its source episode

Content that knows its source episode gets better AI output. In a content item's detail view, set Episode Reference (optional); linking a clip to its episode improves AI caption accuracy. Bulk upload asks the same question up front with Link to source episode.

#Get better results

  • Link every piece of content back to its source episode wherever you're asked, it's the single most reliable way to get sharper AI titles and captions instead of generic ones.
  • Treat a YouTube-imported episode as transcript-only when planning your content mix, it feeds posts and images fine but has no video to cut clips from unless you also upload the file.
  • Use the Episodes filter in the Content Library when you're specifically hunting for source material rather than generated content, it's a much shorter list to scan.
