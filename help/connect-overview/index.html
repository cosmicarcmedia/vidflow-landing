Connect your social accounts

Updated July 9, 2026

Connect your social accounts once per workspace so Vidflow can publish and schedule content on your behalf. This article covers where connections live and what happens as accounts age, expire, or get disconnected. For steps on a specific platform, use the per-platform articles linked below.

#Where to connect

  1. Open Workspace Settings.
  2. Click the Connections tab.
  3. Choose a platform to connect: YouTube, Instagram & Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, or X (Twitter).

Clicking a platform starts that platform's real sign-in flow. You will leave Vidflow briefly, approve access on the platform's own page, and land back in Vidflow once the connection completes.

Every workspace is its own brand. Accounts you connect in one workspace do not appear in another.

#Two kinds of accounts

Vidflow distinguishes accounts that can actually publish from accounts that are only a label:

  • Connected accounts finished the real sign-in flow. These can post and schedule content.
  • Manually added accounts (added by typing a name, or submitted through a client connect link without signing in) are placeholders. They cannot publish until someone completes the sign-in flow for that platform.

If you see a platform pill grayed out when posting, check whether that account was ever actually connected with sign-in.

#Account slots per plan

Each plan allows a limited number of connected social accounts. If you try to connect one more than your plan allows, you will see:

Important

Important: "Your plan allows {limit} connected social account(s). Upgrade your plan in Settings → Billing to connect more."

Reconnecting an account you already have linked never counts against this limit, only brand-new connections do. See Plans and features for your plan's exact allowance.

#Reconnecting

Social platforms expire authorizations on their own schedule, not Vidflow's. When that happens:

  • Vidflow pauses publishing to that account rather than letting posts fail silently.
  • You get an email explaining what happened and what to do.
  • Reconnecting takes you back through the same sign-in flow. Once it succeeds, anything waiting on your schedule or calendar picks back up automatically.

Vidflow refreshes credentials in the background wherever the platform allows it, so most accounts never need a manual reconnect. Reconnects are only needed when the platform itself revokes or expires the authorization. See each platform's article for how long its connection typically lasts.

#Disconnecting

Removing a connected account revokes Vidflow's access to it right away. Anything scheduled to that account will no longer be able to publish until you reconnect it or point the content elsewhere.

#Troubleshooting

A platform I want isn't clickable. The connect buttons only appear for platforms Vidflow has configured. If a platform shows as unavailable, contact support.

I connected an account but posts still fail. Confirm the account shows as connected rather than manually added in the Connections tab. A manually added label cannot publish.

I keep getting reconnect emails for the same account. This usually means the platform is repeatedly expiring the authorization on its end. Reconnect once, and if it recurs quickly, contact support with the account name and platform.
