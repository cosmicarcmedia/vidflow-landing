Caption styles

Updated July 10, 2026

Every clip gets animated, word-timed captions burned into the video. Vidflow ships seven caption styles, from a plain default to bold pop-up text to a boxed style built for busy footage. Pick one when you generate a clip, or switch styles later and re-render.

#How it works

Captions are generated from the clip's word-level transcript, so every word is timed to when it is actually spoken, not just placed on screen for a fixed duration. Each style controls the font, color, position, and animation: some pop each word in one at a time, some fade in whole phrases, one fills karaoke-style as the words are spoken, and one draws a solid background box behind the text so it stands out against anything happening in the footage. Certain words are automatically picked out for emphasis (a punch-word highlight in the accent color), which most styles use to draw the eye to the key phrase in a line.

#The styles

StyleLookBest for
StandardThe house default: clean white-gray uppercase text, centered, no outline, a soft shadow. Punch words highlight in white.A neutral default that works on most footage without calling attention to itself.
Box BackgroundSame clean uppercase text as Standard, but each line sits inside its own solid black rounded box.Light or busy footage where plain white text does not pop on its own.
Bold Center PopLarge bold text, one word at a time, punching in and scaling up as it's spoken. Punch words flash yellow.High-energy moments where you want the captions themselves to feel like part of the pace.
Karaoke BandA bottom bar that fills in word by word, karaoke-style, with punch words highlighted white.Music-adjacent or lyric-style content, or any clip where you want a classic sing-along feel.
Clean MinimalSmall, simple bottom-anchored text with a light blue punch-word highlight.Calmer, more editorial content where you want captions to stay out of the way.
Hype ImpactLarge impact-style text with a red pill highlight around punch words, center-low on the frame.High-energy hooks and reaction-style clips that want maximum visual punch.
Editorial SerifAn elegant serif font in cream and gold, bottom-anchored, italic punch-word highlights.Interview or long-form thought-leadership clips going for a polished, editorial feel.

#Pick a style when generating

  • On the Native tab in the Clip Generator, the Caption style dropdown lists all seven styles. See The Native clip engine.
  • In the clip editor's AI Clip Writing panel, the Caption Style field sets the style used the next time you generate or render captions. See Edit clips: captions, reframing, thumbnails.

#Change the style after generating

Open the clip, pick a new style in the Captions tab or the AI Clip Writing panel, then click Render Edited Clip on the Details tab to burn in the new style. The original render is not overwritten until you do this, so trying a few styles costs nothing but a re-render.

#Get better results

  • Reach for Box Background on light or busy footage. If the shot is bright, has a lot going on behind the speaker, or the text washes out against it, this is the style built to solve that: a solid box makes the text readable no matter what is behind it.
  • Match the style to the platform, not just the footage. Karaoke Band and Bold Center Pop read as high-energy and suit fast-moving short-form feeds; Editorial Serif and Clean Minimal suit slower, more thoughtful content like LinkedIn or a podcast trailer.
  • Try more than one style before settling. Since re-rendering is fast and free of consequence to the original clip, generate with the house default first, then compare it against Box Background or Hype Impact on the same clip before you decide.
  • Keep the source audio clean. Every style times itself to the word-level transcript, so noisy audio that confuses transcription will throw off caption timing regardless of which style you pick.
Note

Note: Free and trial workspaces get a Vidflow watermark and end card on rendered clips, plus a #vidflow hashtag on trial captions. This applies no matter which caption style you choose. Paid plans do not carry the watermark; Starter keeps only the light end card. See Plans and features.
