Connect YouTube

Updated July 9, 2026

Connect YouTube to upload full episodes and publish scheduled clips to your channel from Vidflow.

#What you need before connecting

A Google account with a YouTube channel. You will sign in with that Google account and grant Vidflow permission to upload and read your channel.

#Connect steps

  1. Open Workspace Settings.
  2. Click the Connections tab.
  3. Click YouTube.
  4. Sign in with the Google account that owns your channel and approve access.
  5. You are returned to Vidflow with YouTube listed as connected.

#What Vidflow can publish

YouTube works a little differently from the other platforms:

  • Full episodes publish from the episode's own Publishing tab, not from Post now on library content. You choose the privacy for each upload: Public, Private, or Unlisted. Vidflow defaults new uploads to Private so you can review before making them public.
  • Scheduled clips publish through your regular schedules and queues, and go out as Shorts-eligible videos set to Public.

#Limits enforced

  • Scheduled clip title: 100 characters.
  • Scheduled clip description: 4900 characters.
  • A video file is required.

#Token lifetime and reconnect

Vidflow refreshes your YouTube authorization automatically, about 5 minutes before it would otherwise expire, so most connections never need attention. If Google reports that the authorization itself was revoked, for example if you removed Vidflow's access in your Google account, Vidflow pauses that account and emails you. Reconnect from the Connections tab and anything waiting resumes.

#YouTube's daily upload quota

YouTube enforces its own daily quota separate from your Vidflow plan. If a scheduled post hits that quota, Vidflow does not count it as failed. It automatically reschedules the post for shortly after the next quota reset and tries again, no action needed from you.

#Troubleshooting

YouTube authorization was revoked. You will see "YouTube authorization was revoked. Reconnect your YouTube account in Workspace Settings → Connections." Reconnect from the Connections tab.

A scheduled post to YouTube is stuck in a waiting state. Check whether it is a daily quota delay rather than a failure. Vidflow's note will say the daily quota was reached and that it will retry automatically after the reset.

I expected a private upload and got public, or the reverse. Full episode uploads use the privacy you chose on the Publishing tab. Scheduled clips always publish Public. If you need a clip to stay unlisted or private, publish it manually instead of through a schedule.
