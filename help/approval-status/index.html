Approval status in your library

Updated July 10, 2026

Every content item can carry an approval state, and that state decides whether scheduled publishing will touch it. This article covers the badges, how content enters review, and how approval interacts with publishing.

#How it works

Approval status lives on the content item itself, separate from its publish status, and separate from any specific approval link. A schedule with Require Approval turned on checks that state at dispatch time and holds back anything that isn't Approved; a schedule without it ignores approval state entirely and publishes on schedule regardless. That's also why Post Now can send unreviewed content, it has no schedule attached to check against, so it never looks at approval state at all.

#The badges

  • Needs Approval: the item is waiting for review. Inside the item view the badge reads Pending Approval.
  • Approved: the item has been cleared and publishes normally.
  • Rejected: the item has been marked as not cleared. Rejected items are held back from scheduled publishing the same way pending items are.

You see these badges in the item view in your Content Library and on the client review page. In the library toolbar, the approval filter switches between All Approval States, Needs Approval, and Approved, and the sort menu includes Unapproved First.

Approval state changes when your client acts on the review page: approving an item marks it Approved, and undoing that returns it to Needs Approval.

#Send an item into review

  1. Open the item in your Content Library.
  2. Click Submit for Approval in the footer. The button appears while the item has no approval state yet.
  3. The item is marked Pending Approval.

To put the item in front of a client, add it to an approval link. See Create and share an approval link.

Content can also enter review automatically:

  • Auto-import sources have a require-approval option, so imported files arrive as Needs Approval instead of publishing unseen. See Auto-import from Google Drive or Dropbox.
  • In Flows, the Approvals node routes generated content into review unless it is set to auto-approve. See Flow nodes reference.

#Approval and publishing

Scheduled publishing respects approval state:

  • A scheduled post that is still Needs Approval or Rejected is held back at publish time. Nothing goes out.
  • Once the item is approved, it publishes on the next publishing pass, even if its original time passed while it waited.

Schedules have their own guard: the Require Approval toggle on a schedule means content must be approved before posting through it. See Schedules and queues.

Important

Important: Post Now sends immediately and does not wait for approval. Hold off on it for anything a client still needs to review.

#Get better results

  • Turn on Require Approval on any schedule feeding a client-facing workspace. Approval state alone doesn't stop a schedule without that toggle from publishing unreviewed content, since only schedules with Require Approval on actually check it before dispatching. See Schedules and queues.
  • Sort by Unapproved First when you sit down to review your own queue. It surfaces exactly what's blocking a schedule from moving, instead of you scrolling a full library to find the handful of pending items.
  • Submit content for approval as soon as it's finished, not right before it's due to publish. An item stuck at Needs Approval on its scheduled time simply gets held back, it doesn't publish late on its own. Getting it in front of a client with lead time avoids a scramble.
  • Don't use Post Now as a shortcut around a pending review. It ignores approval state entirely, so it's the one publishing path that can put unapproved content in front of an audience. Reserve it for content that's already cleared.
