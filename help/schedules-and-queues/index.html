Help / Publishing and scheduling

Schedules and queues

Updated July 9, 2026

A schedule automates where and when your content publishes, so you don't have to place every post on the calendar by hand.

#Create a schedule

  1. Open Schedules & Queues.
  2. Click Create Schedule.
  3. Enter a Schedule Name (for example "Daily Instagram Posts").
  4. Select one or more Platforms for this schedule.
  5. Choose a Type: Queue (ordered) or Timeslot (recurring).
  6. Set up the posting schedule (see below) and any filters or toggles you want.
  7. Click Create Schedule.

An empty Schedules & Queues page reads "No schedules yet" with a prompt to create one.

#Queue vs. timeslot

  • Queue (ordered): content is released one at a time, in order, into the next open slot. Good for a steady drip of a backlog.
  • Timeslot (recurring): content publishes at specific recurring times you define, for example every Monday and Thursday at 11:00 AM.

#Setting times

In the posting schedule section, add one or more time rows. Each row has a Time plus day-of-week letter toggles: S M T W R F S, where R stands for Thursday. Click Add another time to add more slots.

If the platform supports it, an Optimal Times panel shows suggested time chips for that platform, which you can click to add directly to the schedule.

#Feed a schedule with tags

Under Filter by Tag, choose one or more tags. Only content carrying at least one of those tags feeds into this schedule automatically. Leave the tag filter empty to manage what's in the schedule manually instead.

#Require Approval

Turn on Require Approval to hold content until it's approved before this schedule will publish it. The toggle description reads: "Content must be approved before posting." This is available on every plan.

Content awaiting approval is skipped by publishing until it's approved. See Client approvals overview and Approval status in your library for how items get approved.

#Recycle Content and Use Optimal Times

Two toggles are marked with a premium badge and require a paid plan:

  • Recycle Content: "Automatically re-queue evergreen content after it has posted." After a recycled item publishes successfully, it's automatically re-scheduled forward on a drip interval instead of retiring, so evergreen posts keep circulating without you re-adding them.
  • Use Optimal Times: "AI-suggested best posting times" for the platform, surfaced as the optimal-time chips mentioned above.

If you're on a plan without access to these, see Plans and features.

#Managing a queue

Open a queue schedule's View / reorder queue action from its card menu to see the items waiting inside it:

  • Each row shows position, thumbnail, title, and time.
  • Use the up/down controls to reorder items, then click Save order.
  • Toggle Shuffle queue to release items in a random order instead of the order added.
  • Shuffle now randomizes the current order immediately (needs at least 2 items).

An empty queue reads "Nothing queued in this schedule yet."

#Editing, pausing, and deleting a schedule

Each schedule card shows an active/paused switch, its platform badge, type badge, and status badge, along with flags for Requires Approval, Recycles Content, and Optimal Times when those are on, plus any tag filters it uses.

From the card's actions menu:

  • Edit Schedule to change any of its settings.
  • Delete Schedule, which confirms: "Are you sure you want to delete {name}? This cannot be undone. Posts already scheduled through this queue will not be affected." Deleting a schedule does not un-schedule content that's already been placed on the calendar through it.

#Troubleshooting

A tagged item isn't publishing: confirm the item actually carries one of the schedule's filter tags, and check whether Require Approval is on and the item hasn't been approved yet.

Recycle Content or Use Optimal Times won't turn on: these require a paid plan. See Plans and features.

Facebook or Instagram posts from a schedule aren't going out: schedules and queues publish automatically to every connected platform, including Facebook and Instagram. If a post still isn't publishing, check its status on the Calendar or in Post statuses and failed posts for a specific failure reason.
