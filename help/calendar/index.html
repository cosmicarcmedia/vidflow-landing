Help / Publishing and scheduling

The Calendar

Updated July 10, 2026

The Calendar is where you see everything scheduled to publish, drag content onto a date and time, and manage or fix individual posts.

#How it works

The Calendar pulls from two places at once: content items scheduled through your schedules and queues, and standalone posts created directly as calendar entries. It merges both into one view, but they aren't dispatched by the same code path, which is why one kind of post (library content on a schedule) can auto-publish to Facebook and Instagram while the other (older calendar queue posts) cannot, see below. Dragging or tapping a slot just sets a date and time; the actual publish happens later, when the scheduled poller reaches that time.

#Views

Open the Calendar and switch views with the switcher at the top: month, week, day, or list. A Today button jumps back to the current date from anywhere.

  • Month view shows up to 3 posts per day cell, with a "+{n} more" link when a day has more.
  • Week and day views show a time grid from 6 AM to 11 PM in 30-minute slots, with a red line marking the current time on today's column.
  • List view groups posts by day (for example "Today · {Month} {Day}") and shows a red Failed badge on any post with a failed status. An empty day reads "Nothing scheduled yet."

#Scheduling content

Drag and drop

Drag a post from the Unscheduled rail onto a day. Holding it over a day for about half a second opens a Select time popover with hour buttons (for example "9 AM") to pick the exact time.

Tap to schedule

On touch devices, tap the post instead of dragging. A banner reads "Tap a day/time slot to schedule {title}." with a Cancel option; tap the destination day or time slot to confirm.

Either method confirms with a toast: "Scheduled for {Month Day} at {time}."

#The Unscheduled rail

The right-hand Unscheduled sidebar lists everything with no publish time yet, with a running count. Each row is labeled "{type} · Library" or "{type} · Queue":

  • Library rows are items sitting in the Content Library with no schedule attached.
  • Queue rows are posts already attached to a schedule's queue, waiting for their next open slot.

Use the search box ("Search posts…") to filter the rail. When everything is scheduled it reads "All posts are scheduled"; when a search matches nothing it reads "No posts match." The rail paginates once you have more than 15 unscheduled items.

A platform legend with colored swatches sits at the bottom of the Calendar so you can identify posts by platform at a glance.

#Editing a scheduled post

Click any post on the calendar to open the Schedule Post dialog. It shows a media preview, a status badge, and platform pills, plus Date and Time fields you can change and save.

From here you can:

  • Delete the scheduled post (confirms with "Delete this scheduled post?").
  • Retry Post, shown on posts that failed.
  • Open in Library, shown when a failure reason indicates the platform isn't supported for automatic dispatch (see below).
  • Edit Original, which takes you to the underlying content in the Library to change title, caption, tags, or media.
  • Cancel or Save your date/time change.

#Facebook and Instagram: know which kind of post you have

Vidflow schedules content two ways, and they behave differently on Facebook and Instagram:

  • Library content scheduled through a schedule or queue (see Schedules and queues) publishes automatically to every platform you've connected, including Facebook and Instagram.
  • Older queue-style posts created directly as calendar queue entries do not auto-publish to Facebook or Instagram. The Calendar flags these with an amber Manual publish required badge and a note: "Facebook and Instagram items can be planned here, but auto-dispatch is not supported yet." To publish one, click Open in Library on the item and publish it from there with Post now.

If you're not sure which kind of post you're looking at, the Manual publish required badge is the signal: if you see it, publish manually from the Library. If you don't see it, the post will go out on its own at the scheduled time.

#Get better results

  • Prefer schedules and queues over dragging one-off posts onto the calendar. Content placed through a schedule auto-publishes to every connected platform, including Facebook and Instagram. Older-style calendar queue entries do not, so building your habit around schedules avoids the manual-publish exception entirely. See Schedules and queues.
  • Use list view for a fast daily failure scan. The red Failed badge is easy to spot scrolling a list of days, faster than checking month view cell by cell.
  • Check the Unscheduled rail before you assume something is missing. Content that's generated but never placed on a date sits there, not on the calendar. A quick search in the rail is often faster than digging back through the Content Library.
  • Reschedule instead of delete-and-recreate. Changing the date and time on an existing scheduled post preserves its caption, media, and platform settings; deleting and starting over throws all of that away for no reason.

#Troubleshooting

A post shows Failed in list view: open it and check the failure detail, then use Retry Post, or Open in Library if the reason points to an unsupported platform for that post type. See Post statuses and failed posts.

A Facebook or Instagram post never went out: check for the Manual publish required badge. If present, it needs to be published manually from the Library, not the Calendar.

No calendar loads at all: make sure a workspace is selected. The Calendar shows "Select a workspace to view the calendar" until one is active.
