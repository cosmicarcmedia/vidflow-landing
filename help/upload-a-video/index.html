Upload a video or audio file

Updated July 9, 2026

Upload a long-form video or audio file and Vidflow saves it as an episode, starts the transcript, and drafts a title, description, and tags in the background.

#Where you can start an upload

  • Click Make Content at the top of the app, then Upload Episode.
  • Click Upload Episode on the Episodes page.
  • If the Episodes view of your Content Library is empty, use its Upload Video button.
  • In the Clip Generator, the Upload file option uploads your file to the library as a new episode, then clips it. See Generate clips from a long video.

#Upload a file

  1. Click Make Content, then Upload Episode.
  2. Drag your file into the dialog, or click Browse files. Vidflow accepts video and audio files: MP4, MOV, MP3, WAV, and M4A.
  3. Keep the tab open while the upload runs. Large files upload in chunks automatically, and the dialog shows progress as each part completes.
Note

Note: Uploads are capped at 1GB per file.

When the upload finishes, Vidflow saves the episode as a draft immediately. Transcription and AI metadata generation (title, description, tags) continue in the background, so you can close the dialog and keep working.

While the upload runs, you can also attach a clipping file with Add a clipping file (optional). This is a clean version of the video without graphics, used later for AI clip generation.

Each upload counts toward your plan's monthly episode allowance. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.

#Start from a YouTube URL instead

The same dialog accepts a YouTube URL. Paste the link and click Auto-fill. Vidflow pulls the video's metadata and transcript, shows you the generated metadata to review, and saves the episode without downloading the video file. To bring in many videos from your own channel at once, see Import from YouTube.

#After the upload

  • The episode appears on the Episodes page and in your Content Library right away.
  • Transcription starts automatically. The notifications bell in the header shows progress. See Transcripts.
  • AI writes a draft title, description, and tags. Open the episode to edit any of them.

#Troubleshooting

Upload paused. If your connection drops mid-upload, the dialog shows which part it stopped at and a Resume button. Click Resume to continue from where it left off instead of starting over.

Stuck on "Finalizing upload". After the last part uploads, the server assembles the file into storage. For large files this can take a few minutes. Leave the tab open until it completes.

"Monthly episode limit reached." You have used your plan's episode allowance for the month. See Usage limits and monthly allowances for what counts and what to do at the cap.

File will not attach. The picker accepts video and audio files only. Export your media as MP4 (video) or MP3 (audio) and try again.
