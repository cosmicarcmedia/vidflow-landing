Help / Publishing and scheduling

Post now

Updated July 9, 2026

Post now sends a single item straight to a connected account, skipping the calendar and queues entirely.

#Where to find it

Post now is available from:

  • The menu on any card in the Content Library.
  • The Edit content modal for a post or clip.
  • The content detail view, opened from a library card.

#Post a piece of content immediately

  1. Open Post now from the card menu, the edit modal, or the detail view.
  2. Choose a platform from the pills shown. Pills only appear for platforms with a connected account: X, LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok.
  3. Review or edit the caption in the text box. A live character counter shows your length against that platform's limit.
  4. If TikTok is selected, fill in the TikTok settings panel (see below).
  5. Click Post now (or the platform-specific send action) to publish.

A successful post shows "Posted to {label}!", with a link to view it when the platform returns one. A failed attempt shows "Publish failed: {message}".

Note

Note: if no accounts are connected, the dialog shows "No connected accounts. Connect one in Workspace Settings > Connected Accounts."

#Platforms and requirements

PlatformVideo requiredCaption/title cap
XNo280 characters
LinkedInNo200 characters (title)
InstagramYes2,200 characters (caption)
FacebookYes255 characters (title), 5,000 characters (description)
TikTokYes150 characters (title)

Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok all post a video. If the item you're posting doesn't have one yet, the dialog tells you: "{Platform} posts a video, and this item doesn't have one yet." Attach or generate a video on the item before posting to those three.

X and LinkedIn can post text-only or with media. X media has a 512MB cap, and video files upload asynchronously, so a video post to X can take up to about two minutes to finish processing.

#YouTube works differently

YouTube does not appear as a pill in Post now. Full episodes publish from the episode itself:

  1. Open the episode from the Content Library.
  2. Go to its Publishing tab.
  3. Upload from the YouTube card there.

See Publish episodes to YouTube and your podcast feed for the full walkthrough. If you want a clip on YouTube instead of the full episode, schedule the clip through a schedule or the calendar rather than using Post now.

#The TikTok settings panel

Selecting TikTok opens a required settings panel before you can post:

  • Posting as {nickname} confirms which connected TikTok account you're using.
  • Who can view this post: the options shown here (Public, Friends, Followers, Only you) come directly from your connected TikTok account, not from Vidflow. If your account restricts an option, it won't appear.
  • Allow comments toggle.
  • Allow Duet and Allow Stitch toggles (video posts only).
  • Disclose video content: turn this on if the post promotes a brand, product, or service. Two checkboxes:
    • Your brand: you're promoting yourself or your own business. Applies the "Promotional content" label.
    • Branded content: you're in a paid partnership or promoting another brand. Applies the "Paid partnership" label.

Branded content can never be posted privately. If you check Branded content and also select Only you, Vidflow blocks the post: "Branded content on TikTok cannot be private. Choose a different audience." You'll also be stopped if you leave the audience unset ("Choose who can view your TikTok post before scheduling it.") or leave disclosure ambiguous ("TikTok disclosure is on: select Your brand or Branded content, or turn disclosure off.").

Posting to TikTok also means agreeing to TikTok's Branded Content Policy and Music Usage Confirmation, noted on the panel itself.

Beta note

Beta note: while Vidflow's TikTok app review is pending, TikTok limits posts to private (Only you) regardless of the audience you choose. Vidflow shows this on the post ("Posted as private (SELF_ONLY): TikTok app pending audit approval.") and records it on the item. This clears automatically once the review completes.

#Troubleshooting

"Publish failed: {message}": the message names the specific platform error. Common causes are an expired connection (reconnect the account in Workspace Settings) or a caption over the platform's limit.

A video-required platform won't let you post: attach a video to the item first, or generate one, then retry.

TikTok won't let you submit: check that you've chosen a viewing audience and resolved the disclosure checkboxes as described above.

Your TikTok post published more private than expected: this is expected during the beta review period. See the beta note above.
