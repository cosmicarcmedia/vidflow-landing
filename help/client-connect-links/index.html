Help / Connecting social accounts

Let clients connect their own accounts

Updated July 9, 2026

A Vidflow Connect link lets someone who owns the social accounts, a client, a talent, a partner brand, connect their own platforms to your workspace without ever signing in to Vidflow itself. You send the link, they sign in with each platform on their end, and the accounts show up ready to publish in your workspace.

Note

Note: Connect links are included on the Pro and Agency plans. See Plans and features.

#Create a link

  1. Open Brand Settings.
  2. Click the Accounts tab.
  3. Click the Vidflow Connect sub-tab.
  4. Type a label for the link, for example the client or brand name.
  5. Click New Connect Link.
  6. Copy the link from the table, or click the preview icon to open it in a new tab.

By default a connect link never expires. Set an expiry in days when you create it if you want the link to stop working after a set window.

#What the recipient sees

When someone opens the link, they land on a page titled Connect Your Social Media Accounts, with a short instruction to sign in with each platform so posts can publish automatically. Two ways to add accounts are offered:

  • Connect with sign-in (recommended): real OAuth buttons for YouTube, Instagram & Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, and X (Twitter). These go through each platform's actual login screen, the same as connecting from inside Vidflow, so the resulting account can publish immediately. Any platform your workspace has not set up shows as Not set up yet and cannot be clicked.
  • Or add manually: label-only entries for anyone who cannot sign in right now. These record an account name and handle but cannot publish until someone comes back and connects with sign-in.

After submitting manually added accounts, the recipient sees Accounts Connected! confirming the accounts were linked to your workspace and that they can close the page. Accounts connected through sign-in return the recipient to the same connect page with a confirmation toast per platform.

#Invalid or expired links

If a link has expired or the token does not match anything on file, the recipient sees Link Not Found, telling them the link is invalid or expired and to ask your team for a new one.

#Managing links

The link table on the Vidflow Connect sub-tab lists every link you have created, with controls to copy the link again, preview it, or delete it. Deleting a link does not remove accounts already connected through it, it only stops the link itself from working.

Important

Important: Anyone with the link can use it to add accounts to your workspace. There is no login required to open it. Share it privately, the same way you would share a password, and avoid posting it anywhere public.

#Troubleshooting

A platform shows "Not set up yet" for my client. That platform is not configured on your workspace yet. Connect it yourself first in Workspace Settings, Connections, then the option becomes available on new connect links (existing open links pick up the change automatically).

My client added an account manually but it will not publish. Manually added accounts are placeholders. Ask them to reopen the link and use Connect with sign-in for that platform instead.

The link says it's invalid, but I recently created it. Check whether an expiry was set and has since passed. Create a new link if needed.
