Blog Post Generator

Updated July 9, 2026

The Blog Post Generator turns an episode, script, or prompt into a long-form HTML article styled for LinkedIn Articles, Substack, or general publishing. You edit the draft directly in the preview, then copy it, download it, or save it to your Content Library.

Find it as Blog Post under Magic Tools in the left rail.

Note: Article generation is available on paid plans. On the free plan the page opens but Generate Article is locked. See Plans and features.

#Generate an article

  1. Pick a source: Episode, Script, or Prompt.
  2. Pick a platform style: LinkedIn Article (professional, high-signal, comment-friendly), Substack (newsletter-style, personal, opinionated), or General (the default).
  3. Pick a tone: Professional, Conversational (the default), Thought Leadership, or Educational.
  4. Pick a length: Short (around 600 words), Medium (around 1,200, the default), or Long (around 2,000).
  5. Add anything specific under Additional Instructions.
  6. Click Generate Article.

With an episode as the source, the article is written from its transcript. The page confirms when a transcript is found and ready, and warns when the episode may not have one yet. See Transcripts.

Note: Articles are written in your brand voice once it is set up; until then the page shows a banner and the AI writes in a generic voice. See Set up your brand voice. Each generation counts toward your plan's monthly AI allowance. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.

#Edit the draft

The preview is directly editable. Click into the article, change the text in place, then use one of the actions below to keep your edits.

#Copy, download, or save

  • Copy HTML puts the article HTML on your clipboard, ready to paste into LinkedIn, Substack, or your CMS.
  • Download exports the article as HTML (.html), Markdown (.md), or plain text (.txt).
  • Save to Library stores the article in your Content Library. A dialog then offers to add it to a queue right away.

#Edit a saved article

Open the article from your Content Library and click Edit in editor. It opens back in the Blog Post Generator, where Save changes updates the saved article.

#Troubleshooting

  • Generate Article is locked with an upgrade prompt. Generation requires a paid plan. See Plans and features.
  • The episode source warns there may be no transcript. Generate the transcript on the episode first, then pick the episode again. See Transcripts.
  • Generation stops with a monthly limit message. You have used this month's AI allowance. See Usage limits and monthly allowances.
