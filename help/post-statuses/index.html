Help / Publishing and scheduling

Post statuses and failed posts

Updated July 10, 2026

Every post in Vidflow carries a status that tells you where it is in the publishing process, plus a separate approval state if approvals apply to it.

#How it works

A background process checks every minute for content whose scheduled time has passed and picks it up for publishing. Each targeted platform is attempted independently, in its own step, so one platform failing doesn't stop the others from being tried. The final status reflects the outcome across all of them: any success marks the post Published, only an across-the-board failure marks it Failed, with the specific per-platform result kept either way so you can see exactly what happened on each one.

#Post statuses

StatusMeaning
DraftNot yet scheduled or sent anywhere.
QueuedSitting in a queue schedule, waiting for its next open slot.
ScheduledPlaced on the calendar with a specific date and time.
PublishingActively being sent to one or more platforms right now.
PublishedSuccessfully sent to at least one platform.
FailedEvery attempted platform failed.

#Approval is a separate dimension

A post also has an approval state, independent of the statuses above: needs approval, approved, or rejected. A schedule with Require Approval turned on will not dispatch an item until it reaches approved. See Client approvals overview and Approval status in your library.

#What "failed" means

Vidflow publishes to every platform an item is set to post on, and it evaluates each platform independently. If every platform attempt fails, the item is marked Failed. If at least one platform succeeds, the item is marked Published, even if others failed.

Either way, the specific error for each platform is kept and shown on the item, in the form:

instagram: {error}; youtube: {error}

So a Published item can still be worth checking: open it to see whether any of its platforms actually failed even though the overall status reads Published.

#Retry a failed post

Click Retry Post on the item, in the Calendar's Schedule Post dialog or from the content card menu. A successful retry shows "Post re-queued" and the item re-enters the normal publishing flow.

#Media links never go dead

Media files are stored with signed links that expire over time. Vidflow re-signs those links at the moment of publish, not when the post was originally scheduled, so a post sitting on the calendar for weeks or months will still find a working file when its time comes.

#Email notifications

Your workspace can list up to three notification email addresses (Workspace Settings > Notifications). Vidflow emails those addresses when a post publishes and when a post fails, according to your workspace's notification settings.

#Get better results

  • Open a Published item once in a while, not just the Failed ones. A partial failure still reads Published overall as long as one platform succeeded, so scanning only for red Failed badges will miss a post that silently didn't reach every platform you targeted.
  • Set all three notification email slots for anything unattended. A workspace you don't check daily benefits most from the publish/failure emails; leaving the list empty means a failure can sit unnoticed until you happen to look.
  • Fix the underlying cause before hitting Retry Post. Retrying a caption that's still over a platform's limit or an account that's still disconnected just fails again the same way. Read the specific per-platform error first, it names the platform and the reason.
  • Don't worry about content sitting scheduled for months. Media links are re-signed at the moment of publish, not when you originally scheduled the post, so a far-out schedule won't hit a dead link on publish day.

#Timezones

Schedules capture the timezone from your browser when you set them up, and times are shown to you in that local timezone. If you or a teammate schedule from a different timezone, keep that in mind when comparing what a given time actually means.

#Troubleshooting

A post is stuck on Publishing: this should resolve quickly. If it doesn't move to Published or Failed after a few minutes, try refreshing, and contact support if it persists.

Published, but something looks wrong: check the per-platform error detail on the item. A partial failure still reads Published overall, with the failing platform's error kept alongside it.

Retry Post doesn't fix it: the error message names the platform and the specific issue. Common causes are an expired account connection (see the platform's connect article), a caption over that platform's limit, or a missing required video. Fix the underlying issue, then retry.

No email arrived for a publish or failure: confirm notification emails are configured in Workspace Settings > Notifications, and check spam.
