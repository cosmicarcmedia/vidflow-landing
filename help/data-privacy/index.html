Your data and privacy

Updated July 10, 2026

You stay in control of the data Vidflow holds. This page covers disconnecting platforms, deleting content, deleting your account, and how Google and YouTube data is handled.

You keep ownership of your content. Vidflow only receives the license it needs to operate the Service on your behalf.

#How it works

Disconnecting a platform or deleting a workspace acts directly on the underlying records, not a soft flag that quietly keeps data around. Disconnecting revokes the stored OAuth token immediately, so Vidflow loses the ability to post or read on your behalf on that platform from that moment on. Freezing a workspace halts every write path for it (no new episodes, clips, or posts), while permanent deletion removes its rows outright. Account-level deletion requests route through a manual, verified process rather than a self-service button, since it touches billing, connected platforms, and every workspace you own at once.

#Disconnect a social platform

  1. Open Workspace Settings and go to the Connections tab.
  2. Under Connected Accounts, disconnect the account.

Disconnecting immediately revokes Vidflow's stored access tokens for that platform and removes the associated profile data.

#Delete a workspace

Open Workspace Settings, then the Danger Zone tab. Two options:

  • Freeze Workspace halts all operations immediately. The workspace is permanently deleted after 90 days unless you unfreeze it.
  • Permanently Delete removes the workspace and all its content: episodes, clips, posts, schedules, and settings. You type the workspace name to confirm, and it cannot be undone.

#Delete your entire account

Email support@vidflow.io from your account's email address and ask for account deletion. Your account and data are deleted within 30 days.

#Request data deletion by email

If you no longer have access to your account, or you want all data associated with you removed, email privacy@vidflow.io with the subject line "Data Deletion Request". Send it from the email address on your account and say which account or connected platform the request covers. Vidflow may ask you to verify ownership first.

For a verified request, personal information is deleted or anonymized within 30 days, except records Vidflow must keep for legal or regulatory reasons.

A deletion request covers:

  • Your account and profile information.
  • OAuth access tokens and profile data from connected platforms.
  • Workspace content: episodes, clips, posts, images, transcripts, and scripts.
  • Usage and log data associated with your account.
Important

Important: content already published to a third-party platform, for example a video posted to your TikTok or Instagram account, is not removed by deleting your Vidflow data. Delete those posts on the platform directly.

#How Google and YouTube data is handled

Vidflow's use of information from Google APIs follows the Google API Services User Data Policy, including its Limited Use requirements.

What Vidflow accesses when you connect Google or YouTube:

  • Basic YouTube channel information: name, ID, thumbnail, and public statistics.
  • Permission to upload and publish to your channel when you publish through Vidflow.
  • Files you choose to import from Google Drive, if you use that feature.

How that data is used:

  • Only to provide the features you request.
  • Never sold.
  • Never used for advertising.
  • Not shared with third parties, except as needed to provide the Service, comply with the law, or as part of a merger or acquisition.

To revoke access, disconnect the account in your workspace settings inside Vidflow, or revoke Vidflow directly in your Google security settings. Once revoked, stored tokens are invalidated and Vidflow no longer receives data from your Google account.

#Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you can request access, correction, deletion, or a portable export of your personal information, unsubscribe from marketing, or withdraw consent by disconnecting integrations. Contact privacy@vidflow.io to exercise any of these rights.

Vidflow does not sell personal information. For full details, read the Vidflow Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

#Troubleshooting

I disconnected a platform but old posts still show it as connected somewhere. Disconnecting revokes the token immediately, but published post history keeps a record of which platform it went to at the time. That is historical record, not a live connection.

I deleted my Vidflow data but content is still visible on TikTok or Instagram. Expected. Vidflow only removes what it holds; anything already published lives on the platform itself and has to be deleted there directly.

I want data removed but no longer have account access. Use the email path: send a Data Deletion Request to privacy@vidflow.io and state which account or platform it covers. Vidflow may ask you to verify ownership before acting on it.
